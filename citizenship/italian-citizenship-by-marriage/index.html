Italian Citizenship by Marriage: The Complete Guide for Americans (2025)

Everything Americans need to know about acquiring Italian citizenship through marriage — eligibility timeline, language requirements, application process, documents, and current wait times.

FB
By Fabrizio Boggio
Updated May 2026 9 min read VERIFIED
✉ 𝕏 ⌘

Marrying an Italian citizen is one of the most accessible pathways to Italian — and EU — citizenship for Americans. Unlike citizenship by descent (which requires proving Italian ancestry) or naturalization (which requires 10 years of residency), citizenship by marriage requires 3 years — either of marriage if you live abroad, or of legal residency in Italy after marriage.

There's a significant catch: the Italian government currently has a multi-year backlog on citizenship by marriage applications, and processing times have stretched to 2–4 years in many cases. That makes timing your application strategically important.

This guide covers the eligibility rules, the full application process, and how to navigate the backlog.

Legal Framework

Italian citizenship by marriage is governed by Law 91/1992, Article 5 — Italy's primary citizenship law.

Key provisions:
- Foreign spouse of an Italian citizen may apply for citizenship after meeting residence/marriage duration requirements
- Application goes to the Prefettura (provincial government office) if living in Italy
- Application goes to the Italian consulate if living abroad
- Citizenship is granted at ministerial discretion — it is not an automatic right (unlike some EU countries)

Reference: L. 91/1992, Articles 5 and 9; D.Lgs. 3/2018 (amendments); Circolare Ministeriale K.28.1 on citizenship procedures

Eligibility Requirements

Time Requirement

You must meet one of these two conditions:

Option A — Living in Italy:
- 3 years of legal residency in Italy after marriage
- "Legal residency" means holding a valid Permesso di Soggiorno + Residenza (Comune registration)
- The 3 years begin from the date your Italian residency was registered, not the marriage date

Option B — Living Abroad:
- 3 years of marriage (if you remain abroad and never establish Italian residency)
- Calculated from the marriage date

Important reduction: These timelines are halved if:
- You have children (biological or adopted) with your Italian spouse — reduces to 18 months (Italy) or 1.5 years (abroad)

Other Requirements

  1. Subsisting marriage — You must still be married at the time of application and at the time citizenship is granted. Divorce ends eligibility.

  2. No serious criminal convictions — Specifically: no convictions for crimes against the Italian state, no crimes carrying 3+ year sentences, no organized crime convictions.

  3. B1 Italian language proficiency — Required by Law 132/2018 (effective December 4, 2018). You must demonstrate B1 level through one of:

  4. Certificate from an authorized institution (Università per Stranieri di Perugia, Società Dante Alighieri, CILS, CELI, PLIDA, AIL)
  5. Italian school diploma
  6. EU language certificate at B1 level or above
  7. Degree from an Italian university

  8. Documented Italian-language schooling

  9. Italian spouse must be Italian citizen — Not just Italian-born; they must currently hold Italian citizenship. If your spouse renounced Italian citizenship, they cannot sponsor your application.

The B1 Language Requirement: What It Means in Practice

B1 is the intermediate level of the Common European Framework of Reference (CEFR). At B1, you can:
- Understand the main points of clear standard speech on familiar matters
- Handle most travel situations in Italy
- Produce simple connected text on familiar topics
- Describe experiences, events, dreams, and ambitions

For most Americans, reaching B1 requires 6–12 months of serious study (3–5 hours/week) or an intensive language course.

Recommended certifications:

Exam Issuing Body Cost Where Taken
CILS B1 Università per Stranieri di Siena €80–150 Authorized centers worldwide
CELI 3 (B1) Università per Stranieri di Perugia €80–130 Authorized centers
PLIDA B1 Società Dante Alighieri €80–120 Dante Alighieri chapters (USA + Italy)
AIL B1 Associazione Italiana Libera €80–100 AIL schools in Italy

Note: The exam must be taken at an authorized testing center. Results are valid indefinitely for citizenship purposes.

If you live in Italy: Enroll in an Italian language school (scuola di italiano per stranieri) at your local university or private school. Many offer exam preparation specifically for citizenship applicants.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Step 1: Verify Eligibility

Confirm:
- ✅ Marriage is legally registered in Italy (see below on marriage registration)
- ✅ 3-year residence OR 3-year marriage requirement met
- ✅ B1 certificate in hand
- ✅ No disqualifying criminal convictions
- ✅ Italian spouse still holds Italian citizenship

Step 2: Register Your Marriage in Italy (If Not Yet Done)

Your U.S. marriage must be officially transcribed (trascritto) into the Italian civil registry. This is done through:
- The Italian consulate in the U.S. (transcription to the municipality where your spouse is registered)
- OR directly at the Ufficio di Stato Civile of the Italian Comune if you're already in Italy

Required: apostilled U.S. marriage certificate + Italian translation

If your spouse is registered at an Italian Comune, the transcription request goes to that Comune. Processing: 2–6 months.

Step 3: Gather Documents

Core documents for the citizenship application:

  1. Domanda di acquisto della cittadinanza italiana — the application form (available at the Prefettura or online portal)
  2. Valid passport — current U.S. passport
  3. Estratto dell'atto di matrimonio — Italian civil registry marriage certificate (from the Comune where it's registered)
  4. Certificato di residenza storico — certificate showing your Italian residency history (from your Comune)
  5. Nulla osta (criminal clearance) from:
  6. U.S. federal: FBI background check, apostilled
  7. U.S. state(s) where you've lived (state police certificates, apostilled)
  8. Any other country where you've lived for 2+ years after age 14
  9. B1 language certificate
  10. Marca da bollo (€16 revenue stamp — from any tabacchi)
  11. Application fee: €250 (paid via F24 payment form)

Document translations: All non-Italian documents need certified Italian translation by a sworn translator (traduttore giurato).

Step 4: Submit the Application

If living in Italy: Submit to the Prefettura of your province. Many Prefetture now use an online portal (portale cittadinanza.interno.gov.it) for initial submission, followed by in-person document delivery.

If living abroad: Submit to the Italian consulate in your U.S. consulate district.

Online portal: cittadinanza.interno.gov.it

Step 5: Wait

This is where patience is required. Current processing times (2024–2025):

  • Official legal deadline: The Ministero dell'Interno has 730 days (2 years) from application to decide
  • Actual experience: Many applicants wait 2–4 years
  • The backlog has grown significantly due to pandemic-era delays and increased applications

During the wait:
- Keep your residency current (renew Permesso, maintain Residenza)
- Keep your marriage intact
- You'll receive no updates unless requested — applicants can check status at the Prefettura or via the online portal

Step 6: Decree and Oath

When approved, you receive a Decreto di Concessione della Cittadinanza (citizenship grant decree). You must then:

  1. Take the Oath of Allegiance (giuramento) — administered at the Prefettura (or consulate if abroad). Must be taken within 6 months of the decree date or the decree expires.

  2. Receive your Italian passport — after the oath, apply at your Questura or Comune for an Italian passport and ID card.

After Citizenship: EU Rights

Italian citizenship is EU citizenship. Once you're a citizen, you can:
- Live and work anywhere in the EU without a visa
- Vote in Italian elections (and European Parliament elections)
- Pass citizenship to your children (jure sanguinis — citizenship by descent through you)
- Travel on an Italian/EU passport — visa-free access to 186+ countries
- Retire anywhere in the EU on the same terms as citizens of that country

You do not lose U.S. citizenship by acquiring Italian citizenship. The U.S. does not require renunciation in this case. You'll be a dual citizen.

Common Problems and Solutions

Marriage Not Registered in Italy

Problem: You got married in the U.S. and never registered it in Italy. Citizenship applications cannot proceed without Italian-registered marriage records.

Solution: File the transcription request immediately at the consulate or Comune. This can take 2–6 months. Start this before you start collecting other documents.

Expired FBI Background Check

Problem: FBI checks are typically valid for 6 months. If your application takes years to process, the check expires.

Solution: Many applicants refresh the FBI check 1–2 years after initial submission as the Prefettura approaches the decision. The Prefettura may request updated documentation during processing.

Divorce During Processing

Problem: Marriage dissolves after application submitted but before decision.

Solution: You lose eligibility. The citizenship application is void. You would need to re-establish eligibility through a different pathway (10-year naturalization, for example).

B1 Exam Failure

Problem: Failed the B1 exam before application. Some applicants submit applications without B1 and hope to add it later.

Solution: You cannot submit a complete application without B1. Take the exam before applying. If you fail, most exam centers allow retakes within 3–6 months.

Citizenship by Marriage vs. Other Pathways: Comparison

Pathway Time Required Language Req. Key Condition
Marriage (in Italy) 3 years residency B1 Must remain married
Marriage (abroad) 3 years of marriage B1 Must remain married
Naturalization 10 years residency B1 No special condition
Jure Sanguinis No time req. None Must prove Italian ancestry
Jure Sanguinis (1948) No time req. None Maternal line before 1948

For Americans married to Italians, the marriage pathway is almost always faster than naturalization. The 3-year timeline (vs. 10 years for naturalization) makes it the dominant choice.

Current Wait Times and Strategic Timing

Given the 2–4 year wait, here's how to think about timing:

Apply as soon as you're eligible. Don't wait for "the right moment." Every month you delay is a month added to the wait.

Apply with a complete file. Incomplete applications are returned for correction, adding months. Get every document right before submitting.

Notify the Prefettura of any changes. Changed address, new passport, renewed Permesso — update your file proactively.

Consider an immigration lawyer for complex cases — dual prior marriages, criminal history questions, descent issues. Cost: €500–2,000 for citizenship by marriage case. Worth it if anything in your file is non-standard.
