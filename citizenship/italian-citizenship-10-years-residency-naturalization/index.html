Italian Citizenship by 10 Years of Residency: The Naturalization Route for Americans (2025)

After 10 consecutive years of legal Italian residence, Americans can apply for citizenship by naturalization. B1 Italian required, €250 fee, 2–4 year processing time. Italy does not require you to renounce your US passport.

Quick Answer

Americans who have lived legally in Italy for 10 consecutive years can apply for Italian citizenship by naturalization (cittadinanza per naturalizzazione). Requirements: 10 years of uninterrupted legal residence, income above approximately €8,300/year, Italian language at B1 level, clean criminal record. The application is submitted online via the Interior Ministry portal (portaleintegrazione.it) and currently takes 2–4 years to process. Italy allows dual citizenship — you keep your US passport.

Why Pursue Italian Citizenship?

After 10 years of living in Italy, citizenship by naturalization opens doors that even the permesso di lungo periodo cannot:

  • EU citizenship: freedom to live, work, retire, and study anywhere in all 27 EU member states without visas
  • Italian passport: visa-free or visa-on-arrival access to 190+ countries
  • No more renewals: never renew a permesso di soggiorno again
  • Full political rights: vote in Italian (and EU) elections
  • Dual citizenship: Italy does not require you to renounce US citizenship

The 10-Year Requirement: What Counts

The 10 years must be continuous legal residence:

  • You must have been registered in the anagrafe (Italian population register) for the entire period
  • Your permesso di soggiorno must have been valid throughout — no gaps without a valid ricevuta

Short absences are permitted. Italian law allows absences of up to 6 consecutive months or 10 months total during the 10 years without breaking the residency requirement.

Which residency years count:

  • Years as an elective resident: ✅
  • Years as a digital nomad visa holder: ✅
  • Years as a student: ✅ (document carefully)
  • Years without valid permesso (even if physically present): ❌

Full Requirements for Naturalization

Income Threshold

For the 3 years preceding the application:

  • Single applicant: approximately €8,300/year (2025 — updated annually)
  • With spouse: approximately €11,100/year
  • With spouse and one child: approximately €13,300/year

This is significantly lower than the Elective Residence Visa minimum — most Americans already far exceed it.

Italian Language at B1 Level

Since December 2018, naturalization applicants must demonstrate Italian language ability at B1 level (intermediate level).

How to prove B1: Pass the official CELI 3 (B1) exam by Università per Stranieri di Perugia; pass the CILS B1 exam by Università per Stranieri di Siena; hold an Italian educational qualification.

💡 Tip

Register for the B1 exam in year 7–8 of your Italian residence, before you need it. Exam sessions are limited (typically twice per year) and spots fill up. Do not delay your citizenship application because you couldn't get an exam slot.

Clean Criminal Record

No Italian criminal convictions for certain categories of crimes. No foreign criminal convictions for serious offenses. Some crimes (terrorism, organized crime, crimes against the state) are absolute bars.

Active Renunciation (Not Required for Americans)

Italy does not require you to renounce your US citizenship. The USA also generally does not revoke citizenship for naturalizing in a foreign country when you do not intend to relinquish US citizenship. Americans routinely hold dual US-Italian citizenship.

The Application Process

Step 1: Gather Documents

  • Valid passport (copies of all pages)
  • Current permesso di soggiorno or lungo periodo
  • Codice fiscale
  • Certificato storico di residenza (historical residence certificate showing 10+ years) — from your Comune
  • All permesso di soggiorno documents (every permit since first arrival)
  • Italian tax returns (dichiarazioni dei redditi) for last 3 years
  • Italian criminal record certificate (from your Tribunale)
  • FBI Identity History Summary with apostille and Italian translation
  • CELI or CILS B1 certificate
  • Marriage certificate (apostilled + translated) if married

Step 2: Submit Application Online

Applications are submitted electronically via the Portale Integrazione Migranti (portaleintegrazione.it). The portal requires digital copies of all documents, electronic payment of the application fee (€250), and a valid email address for correspondence. Once submitted, you receive an application number (numero di pratica) to track your application.

Step 3: Municipal Verification

Your local Prefettura and Questura conduct investigations: verifying your residency history with your Comune, checking criminal records, and verifying income documentation. You may be called for an interview or asked for additional documents.

Step 4: Presidential Decree

Citizenship is granted by Presidential Decree (Decreto del Presidente della Repubblica). This is the bottleneck — it requires the President's signature after review by the Ministry of Interior.

Current processing time: 2–4 years from application. The Ministry is legally required to process within 2 years, but this deadline is routinely exceeded.

⚠ Important

Submit your application as soon as you are eligible (at the 10-year mark). Do not wait for "a better time" — the queue is long and starts from the day you submit.

Step 5: Oath of Loyalty

When your application is approved, you appear before your local Comune to swear an oath of loyalty to the Italian Republic (giuramento di fedeltà). Citizenship takes effect from the date of the oath, not the decree.

After Citizenship: Getting Your Italian Passport

  1. Register your citizenship at your Comune's anagrafe (automatic upon oath)
  2. Apply for the carta d'identità (Italian ID card) at your Comune
  3. Apply for the Italian passport at your Comune's Sportello Passaporti or a designated Questura office

Passport processing time: 30–90 days. Italian passport benefits: visa-free/visa-on-arrival access to 190+ countries, right to live and work in all 27 EU member states.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will the US revoke my American citizenship if I become Italian?
No. The US does not revoke citizenship for voluntarily naturalizing in most foreign countries (Italy included) when you do not intend to relinquish US citizenship. Verify current US law — consult the US State Department or a US immigration attorney.

Can I count years as a student toward the 10 years?
Generally yes — student visa holders registered in the Italian anagrafe accumulate residency years. Document your academic activity carefully.

What if my application is denied?
You can appeal (ricorso) to the TAR (Tribunale Amministrativo Regionale) within 60 days of denial. Consult an Italian administrative lawyer.

My spouse is Italian — do I have a faster path?
Yes. Non-Italian spouses of Italian citizens have a faster path (3 years of marriage and residence instead of 10 years), governed by separate rules.
