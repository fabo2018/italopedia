Cookie Policy

Italopedia uses minimal cookies. This page explains exactly which, why, and how to control them.

Last updated: May 15, 2026

1. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files that websites place on your device to remember things. Some are useful (so you don't have to log in every visit); some are invasive (so advertisers can follow you across the internet).

2. Italopedia's approach

We use the minimum possible. No advertising cookies. No tracking pixels. No third-party data brokers. Our analytics provider (Plausible) does not use cookies at all.

3. What cookies we actually set

4. Cookies we don't set

No Google Analytics. No Google Ads. No Facebook Pixel. No Hotjar. No marketing remarketing pixels. No data-broker cookies.

5. How to control cookies

You can block or delete cookies via your browser settings. Essential cookies are required for the shop to function — blocking them will prevent purchases.

6. Changes to this policy

The current version is always at this URL. Material changes will be announced in the newsletter.
