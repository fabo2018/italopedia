The move, in order

Your roadmap from US to IT

Most Americans get the sequence wrong. The visa is not step one — the income proof and the FBI background check are. Here's the real order.
Month -12 to -6
Choose your visa pathway
Match your income type to the right visa: elective residence, digital nomad, student, investor, or citizenship route.
Month -6 to -4
Documents & apostilles
FBI background check, US tax returns, proof of income, health insurance, accommodation in Italy.
Month -4 to -2
Consulate appointment
Book early — popular consulates run 3–6 months out. Bring originals plus translations.
Month 0
Arrive in Italy
8-day declaration of presence. Kit postale within 60 days. Codice fiscale if you don't have it yet.
Month +1 to +6
Become a resident
Anagrafe registration, tessera sanitaria, Italian bank account, electric/gas/internet contracts.
Year +1 onwards
Tax filing & renewal
First Italian tax return (Modello Redditi), permesso renewal annually, US filing obligations continue.

Editorial · Field notes from Piedmont

What it's actually like

Short dispatches from real life in Italy — the things our long-form guides can't quite hold. Updated weekly.
Bureaucracy · No. 142

The questura kept my fingerprints. They will not be returned.

"At the questura today: the officer stamps your kit postale slip, takes ten prints, and hands you a small sheet of paper. That paper — the cedolino — is your only proof of residency status for the next three to eight months. Lose it and you start over."

Note 142 · May 18, 2025 · Cuneo
Money · No. 141

Why your American card got declined at the SIM card kiosk

"Italian point-of-sale terminals frequently reject US cards that lack a chip-and-PIN configuration. The fix is usually nothing — just hand them the same card again, and the terminal falls back. But the first time, in a small town, with the line growing, you will sweat through your shirt."

Note 141 · May 14, 2025 · Alba
Healthcare · No. 140

The blood draw appointment took twelve days. The result took twenty-eight minutes.

"Italian public healthcare is not slow once you're inside it. The slowness is the queue. Once you have the appointment, once the prelievo is done, the result is usually digital within an hour. This is a country that hands you your blood panel before lunch and the prescription before dinner."

Note 140 · May 9, 2025 · Torino
