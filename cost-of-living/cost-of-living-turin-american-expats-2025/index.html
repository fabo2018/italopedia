Cost of Living in Turin for American Expats: Real 2025 Numbers

Turin is one of Italy's most affordable major cities — and one of the most overlooked by American expats. Here are the real numbers: rent by neighborhood, groceries, dining out, transport, and complete monthly budgets for singles and couples. Updated Q2 2025.

Quick Answer

A single person can live comfortably in Turin on €1,400–€1,800/month including rent. A couple can live very well on €2,200–€2,800/month. Compared to Rome or Milan, Turin offers similar urban amenities at rental costs 30–40% lower. Compared to New York, San Francisco, or Los Angeles, Turin is 50–65% cheaper overall.

Why Turin?

Turin (Torino) is Italy's fourth-largest city and one of its most underrated by expats. The city offers:

  • A major university (Politecnico di Torino, ranked in the top 200 globally)
  • World-class museums (Museo Egizio — second largest Egyptian museum in the world; Museo Nazionale del Cinema)
  • Excellent food culture (birthplace of Slow Food, home of Lavazza and Ferrero)
  • Alps access: 1 hour to ski resorts
  • Direct flights to major European hubs
  • Substantially lower cost of living than Rome, Milan, or Florence
  • Less touristy, more authentically Italian daily life

Best Neighborhoods for American Expats

Crocetta: Elegant, quiet, good schools. Popular with professionals and retired expats. Slightly higher rents.

San Salvario: Vibrant, multicultural, great restaurant and bar scene. Popular with younger expats and digital nomads. Good value. The sweet spot for most Americans — central, walkable, affordable, with a strong expat community.

Vanchiglia / Aurora: Up-and-coming neighborhoods with good value and growing expat communities. Some parts still gritty — choose streets carefully.

Borgo Po / Precollina: Residential areas near the hills. Quieter, greener, slightly outside center. Excellent for families.

Lingotto / Nizza Millefonti: Former industrial area now modern and well-connected. More affordable, practical over prestigious.

The Porta Palazzo market (one of Europe's largest open-air markets) is in central Turin every morning Monday–Friday and all day Saturday. Fresh produce, cheese, meat, and local specialties at prices significantly below supermarkets. Typical monthly grocery cost for a couple: €350–€500.

💡 Turin's Aperitivo Culture

Turin invented the aperitivo. Between 6–9 PM, most bars offer a complimentary buffet with your drink purchase. For €7–10, you get a Campari or Prosecco plus access to a spread of snacks, pasta, and appetizers. Many locals and expats make this their dinner several nights a week. The Quadrilatero Romano and San Salvario are the best areas — go early (6:30 PM) for the freshest buffet.

GTT is Turin's transit operator. The metro (one line) connects the southern suburbs to the center. Trams and buses cover the rest comprehensively. Most expats do not need a car.

Healthcare

As a registered resident with SSN access:

  • GP visits: free
  • Specialist visits (with GP referral): €30–€50 co-pay (ticket)
  • Emergency room: free (or small co-pay)
  • Prescription medications: €1–€10 co-pay typically

Private healthcare: GP visit €60–€120; specialist €100–€200. International health insurance: €150–€400/month depending on age and coverage.

Practical Tips for Reducing Costs in Turin

  1. Shop at Lidl and the Porta Palazzo market — your grocery bill can be 40% lower than at Esselunga
  2. Use the annual transit pass — €380/year vs. €456 if buying monthly
  3. Embrace aperitivo culture — €8 gets you a drink and dinner-quality food several nights a week
  4. Negotiate rent — for longer-term leases (1+ year), landlords often accept 5–10% below asking
  5. Get voluntary SSN enrollment — at ~€2,000/year, it's cheaper than most international health insurance
