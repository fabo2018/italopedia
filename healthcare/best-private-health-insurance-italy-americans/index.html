Best Private Health Insurance for Americans in Italy (2025): Cigna vs AXA vs Allianz

The best international health insurance plans for Americans living in Italy — detailed comparison of Cigna Global, AXA, Allianz Care, and others. Costs, coverage, and when to combine with Italy's SSN.

Every American moving to Italy needs international health insurance — first as a requirement for your visa application, then as practical protection before you enroll in Italy's national health service (SSN), and potentially as a supplement to the SSN indefinitely.

Why Private Health Insurance in Italy

You Need It for Your Visa

The Elective Residency Visa and Digital Nomad Visa both require proof of health insurance valid in Italy with minimum coverage of €30,000.

You Need It While Waiting for SSN Enrollment

Getting enrolled in Italy's national health service takes weeks to months after arrival. Private insurance covers the gap.

Many Expats Keep It Even After SSN Enrollment

Italy's SSN has limitations:

  • Specialist appointment waits of 4–8 weeks
  • Limited English-speaking doctors
  • No coverage when you travel outside Italy

GeoBlue (BCBS International) — Best for Americans who need US coverage. Premium option for people who return to the US frequently.

What to Look For: Key Features Checklist

Coverage must-haves:

  • Minimum €30,000 coverage (visa requirement)
  • Hospital inpatient coverage
  • Emergency evacuation
  • Coverage valid throughout Italy
  • Pre-existing conditions policy (check carefully)

Watch out for:

  • High deductibles that make small claims worthless
  • Geographic exclusions
  • Plans that don't include Italy in their core coverage area

The Smart Strategy: Layering SSN + Private

Year 1 (pre-SSN enrollment): Full private international plan — this is your primary coverage.

After SSN enrollment:

  • Keep a basic/mid-tier private plan for travel coverage and faster specialist access
  • Rely on SSN for primary care, hospitalizations, and regular GP visits
  • Total cost: SSN (~€167/month) + private (~$100–200/month) = ~€300–400/month total healthcare

FAQ

Does my US health insurance work in Italy? Almost certainly not for long-term expatriate coverage. Most US plans cover emergency care abroad to some degree, but they don't cover routine care or extended stays.

Does Medicare cover me in Italy? No. Medicare does not cover healthcare outside the United States (with very limited exceptions).

What's the minimum coverage needed for the Elective Residency Visa? The Italian government requires minimum coverage of €30,000 valid throughout Italy for the duration of your stay.
