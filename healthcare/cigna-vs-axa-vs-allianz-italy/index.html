Cigna vs AXA vs Allianz: Which International Health Insurance Is Best for Italy? (2025)

Head-to-head comparison of Cigna Global, AXA International, and Allianz Care for Americans living in Italy — real coverage details, claims experience, Italy-specific network, and which wins by age and budget.

FB
By Fabrizio Boggio
Updated May 2026 8 min read VERIFIED
✉ 𝕏 ⌘

You've narrowed it down to the big three. Cigna, AXA, and Allianz dominate the international health insurance market for American expats in Italy — and for good reason. All three are financially stable, widely accepted by Italian consulates, and have direct billing relationships with Italian private hospitals.

But they're not the same. The differences matter significantly depending on your age, health history, how much time you spend in the U.S., and what you need from insurance beyond the visa minimum.

I'm based in Piedmont and have tracked the experience of Americans in Italy with all three providers for years. Here's the honest comparison.

The Short Answer (If You're in a Hurry)

  • Best overall / most recognized: Cigna Global
  • Best value under 55: AXA International
  • Best value over 55: Allianz Care
  • Best if you travel to the U.S. frequently: GeoBlue (not in the top 3, but worth noting)
  • Best for families: AXA (family discounts, solid pediatric coverage)

Now the details.

Coverage Architecture: How Each Is Structured

Cigna Global

Cigna uses a modular build approach. You start with a core inpatient plan and add modules:
- Core (Silver): Inpatient + emergency
- + Outpatient module: GP visits, specialist visits, diagnostics
- + Mental Health module
- + Dental + Vision module
- + International Life Assistance (24/7 nurse line, second opinions)

This modularity is Cigna's biggest advantage — you pay only for what you actually use. A healthy 35-year-old remote worker might take Core + Outpatient and skip dental. A 62-year-old with complex health history takes everything.

Maximum benefit: Unlimited (on all tiers — important)

AXA International

AXA uses tiered plans (Essential, Comprehensive, Premium) — less flexible than Cigna but simpler to understand:
- Essential: Inpatient only
- Comprehensive: Inpatient + outpatient + mental health
- Premium: Everything including dental, vision, wellness

Maximum benefit: Unlimited on Comprehensive and Premium; €1.5M on Essential

Allianz Care

Allianz uses a similar tiered structure:
- ClassicSmart: Inpatient + emergency
- ClassicFlex: Inpatient + outpatient
- MedicalPlus: Full coverage

Maximum benefit: Unlimited on MedicalPlus; €2M on ClassicFlex and ClassicSmart

These are mid-tier equivalent comparisons (inpatient + outpatient, no dental/vision) for a single American enrolling with no significant pre-existing conditions, Italy as primary location:

Key takeaway: The Allianz price advantage grows with age. At 70, Allianz is 35% cheaper than Cigna for equivalent coverage. At 30, the difference is smaller and brand recognition may matter more.

Italy-Specific Hospital Network

All three have direct billing relationships with major Italian private hospitals. This means you don't pay out of pocket and wait for reimbursement — the insurer pays the hospital directly.

Direct Billing Networks in Italy

Cigna Global:
Most extensive Italian network. Includes:
- Humanitas (Milan, Bologna, Torino)
- GVM Care & Research (nationwide)
- Policlinico San Donato (Milan)
- Villa Maria (nationwide Catholic hospital network)
- Ospedale San Raffaele (Milan)
- Gemelli (Rome)

AXA International:
Solid network, slightly smaller than Cigna:
- Humanitas group
- GVM network
- Major university hospitals in Florence, Rome, Turin
- Smaller direct billing presence in southern Italy

Allianz Care:
Adequate network for major cities; thinner coverage in smaller towns and southern Italy:
- Main Allianz Care partner hospitals in Milan, Rome, Florence, Turin
- Less comprehensive in Sicily, Sardinia, Calabria

Practical implication: If you live in Milan, Florence, Rome, or Turin, all three work well. If you live in rural Tuscany, Umbria, or southern Italy, Cigna's broader network gives you more options.

Claims Process: The Real-World Test

This is where insurers reveal their true character. What happens when you actually need to use the insurance?

Cigna Global Claims Experience

Direct billing (cashless): Works reliably at network hospitals. Show your Cigna card, the hospital bills Cigna directly, you pay only any applicable co-pay or deductible.

Reimbursement claims: Submit via the MyCigna app or online portal. Average processing time: 10–20 business days for standard claims. Complex claims longer.

English support: 24/7 English-language customer service. Italian-specific team available. This is Cigna's strongest operational advantage — if you have a complex claim, you're talking to someone who speaks English and understands Italian healthcare.

Common complaint: Premium increases at renewal can be significant — 10–20% annual increases are reported by long-term policyholders.

AXA International Claims Experience

Direct billing: Reliable at major network hospitals. Slightly more paperwork than Cigna at smaller facilities.

Reimbursement: Online portal (MyAXA) is functional but less polished than Cigna. Processing time: 15–25 business days.

English support: Business hours English service. Less 24/7 than Cigna. Some users report difficulty reaching resolution on complex claims.

Common complaint: Initial documentation requests can be extensive. First claim with AXA often takes longer as they establish your file.

Allianz Care Claims Experience

Direct billing: Works well at partner facilities. Outside the network, you pay upfront and claim reimbursement.

Reimbursement: Submit via the Allianz Care app. Straightforward for standard claims. Processing: 10–20 business days.

English support: International team with English speakers. Less Italy-specific expertise than Cigna.

Common complaint: Pre-authorization requirements on some procedures are stricter than Cigna or AXA — get approval before elective treatments.

Pre-Existing Conditions: Critical Comparison

Cigna's Approach

  • Moratorium option: 2-year exclusion then coverage if symptom-free
  • Full medical underwriting: Available — declare everything, get explicit confirmation of what's covered
  • Chronic conditions: Available for coverage with medical underwriting at premium loading
  • Mental health: Covered on Gold and Platinum plans; not on Silver

AXA's Approach

  • Moratorium standard: 2-year exclusion
  • Full underwriting: Available on request
  • Chronic conditions: Handled case-by-case through underwriting
  • Mental health: Included in Comprehensive and Premium plans

Allianz's Approach

  • Moratorium standard: 2-year exclusion
  • Full underwriting: Available
  • Chronic conditions: More restrictive permanent exclusions possible
  • Mental health: Covered on MedicalPlus; limited on lower tiers

Winner for complex medical history: Cigna — most transparent underwriting process, most willing to insure complex cases with explicit terms.

What Each Provider Does Best

Cigna Wins On:

  • ✅ Most comprehensive Italian hospital network
  • ✅ Best English-language claims support
  • ✅ Most recognized by Italian consulates
  • ✅ Unlimited lifetime benefit on all tiers
  • ✅ Best for complex medical histories

AXA Wins On:

  • ✅ Best price-to-coverage ratio under 55
  • ✅ Family discount (best for couples/families)
  • ✅ Simpler plan structure
  • ✅ Strong brand recognition in Europe

Allianz Wins On:

  • ✅ Best pricing for 55+ and 65+
  • ✅ Competitive senior rates where Cigna and AXA spike
  • ✅ Pre-authorization process protects against surprise bills
  • ✅ Strong claims app

Recommended Picks by Profile

Young remote worker (25–40), healthy, budget-conscious:
→ AXA Comprehensive — 20% cheaper than Cigna for same tier, solid coverage, simpler administration.

Retiree (60–70) with moderate health needs:
→ Allianz Care ClassicFlex — 25–35% cheaper than Cigna at this age, adequate Italian network for most locations.

Retiree with complex health history or on multiple medications:
→ Cigna Gold with full medical underwriting — pay more but know exactly what's covered before you need it.

Family with children:
→ AXA Premium — family discounts, good pediatric coverage, comprehensive plan.

High earner wanting top-tier everything:
→ Cigna Platinum — comprehensive, prestigious, expensive. Worth it for peace of mind if budget isn't the constraint.

Someone splitting time Italy + U.S.:
→ GeoBlue Xplorer Premier — includes U.S. BCBS network which neither Cigna, AXA, nor Allianz includes by default.

The SSN Transition Plan

Regardless of which provider you choose initially, plan your transition to Italy's SSN:

  1. Months 1–6: Private international insurance (required for visa)
  2. Month 6–12: Establish residency, enroll in SSN, get GP assigned, learn the system
  3. After year 1: Evaluate whether to drop private insurance, keep a supplement only, or maintain full private coverage

The SSN at €387–1,500/year is dramatically cheaper than any private option. The question is whether the trade-off in service speed and specialist access is acceptable for your situation.

How to Get Quotes

All three providers quote online:
- Cigna: cignaglobal.com/plans
- AXA: axaglobalhealthcare.com
- Allianz: allianzcare.com

Use a broker (Pacific Prime, Expat Insurance, William Russell) to compare quotes across providers simultaneously — brokers receive commission from insurers, so their comparison service is free to you. A good broker will also help negotiate terms and flag policy exclusions.
