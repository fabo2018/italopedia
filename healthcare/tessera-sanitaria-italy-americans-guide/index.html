Tessera Sanitaria: How to Get Italy's Health Card as an American (2025)

Complete guide for Americans on how to get the Italian Tessera Sanitaria (health card) — including SSN enrollment, ASL registration, costs updated for 2025, choosing a GP, and private insurance options.

The Tessera Sanitaria is Italy's national health insurance card — the plastic card that gives you access to Italy's public healthcare system (SSN — Servizio Sanitario Nazionale). Without it, you pay full private rates for every doctor visit, prescription, and hospital stay.

For Americans used to the US healthcare system, Italy's SSN is a revelation: GP visits are free, specialist visits cost €15–36 in co-pays, prescriptions are heavily subsidized, and hospital care is essentially free.

What Is the Tessera Sanitaria?

A plastic card — similar in size to a credit card — issued by Italy's national health authority. It serves as proof of your enrollment in Italy's national health service.

What it gives you access to:

  • Free GP (medico di base) visits
  • Subsidized specialist visits (co-pay of €15–36)
  • Subsidized prescription medications (co-pay of €0–8)
  • Public hospital care
  • Emergency treatment
  • Subsidized diagnostic tests

Before You Can Get the Tessera Sanitaria: Prerequisites

You need three things in place:

  1. Codice Fiscale — your Italian tax ID number
  2. Permesso di Soggiorno (or at minimum, your postal ricevuta)
  3. Registered address — either your Anagrafe registration confirmation or a signed registered lease

Mandatory vs. Voluntary Enrollment

Mandatory Enrollment (Free)

Applies to work visa holders, family reunification permit holders, asylum seekers, and certain special categories.

Voluntary Enrollment (Annual Fee)

Americans on Elective Residency Visas fall under voluntary enrollment. You pay an annual contribution.

2025 costs — important update: Italy's 2024 Budget Law dramatically increased voluntary SSN fees effective January 2024.

Students (study visa holders): €700/year (separate reduced rate)

Step-by-Step: How to Get Your Tessera Sanitaria

Step 1: Get Your Codice Fiscale

Required first.

Step 2: Apply for Your Permesso di Soggiorno

The ASL can accept your postal ricevuta. You don't need the physical permit card.

Step 3: Confirm Your Registered Address

Either your Anagrafe registration confirmation or your signed, registered lease agreement.

Step 4: Pay the Annual SSN Contribution (Voluntary Enrollment Only)

  1. Get the F24 form (download from agenziaentrate.gov.it or at any tabaccheria)
  2. Fill in using tax code 8846 for the SSN contribution
  3. Pay at any bank, post office, or online via F24 portal
  4. Keep the receipt — you'll need it at the ASL

Step 5: Visit Your Local ASL Office

What to bring:

  • Valid US passport
  • Codice Fiscale
  • Permesso di Soggiorno card or ricevuta
  • Proof of address
  • F24 payment receipt (for voluntary enrollment)

Step 6: Receive Your Temporary Certificate + Choose Your GP

At the ASL, you'll receive a temporary paper certificate that works immediately and choose your Medico di Base (GP).

💡 Note

Choosing your GP is important. This doctor becomes your primary healthcare contact for everything — referrals, prescriptions, certificates. Look for one who speaks English if possible.

Step 7: Receive Your Physical Tessera Sanitaria by Mail

The physical plastic card is mailed to your registered address within 2–8 weeks of enrollment.

Annual Renewal

Your SSN enrollment runs January 1 to December 31 of each calendar year. Pay the F24 contribution again for the new year and bring the receipt + updated documents to the ASL.

How Italy's Public Healthcare Actually Works

Your GP (Medico di Base)

  • Free visits — no co-pay for GP consultations
  • Referrals (impegnative) — to see a specialist, you generally need a referral
  • Prescriptions — subsidized medications

Specialist Visits

With a GP referral, book through the CUP system. Co-pay: €15–36 per visit. Wait times: 2–8 weeks for routine visits.

Emergency Care

Dial 118 for ambulance. Emergency room co-pay of approximately €25 for non-urgent visits.

FAQ

Can I enroll in the SSN before my Permesso di Soggiorno arrives? Yes — the postal ricevuta is sufficient for ASL enrollment.

Is €2,000/year worth it compared to keeping private insurance? For long-term residents, usually yes — the SSN provides comprehensive coverage including hospital care. But for stays under 1 year, private insurance may be more economical.

Does my spouse need their own SSN enrollment? If your spouse is fiscally dependent on you, they can be added to your enrollment at no extra cost.
