Tessera Sanitaria: How to Get Italy's Health Card as an American (2025)

Italy's tessera sanitaria is your codice fiscale card, national health service enrollment card, and European Health Insurance Card all in one. As a registered resident, it arrives automatically. Here is everything you need to know. Updated 2025.

Quick Answer

The tessera sanitaria is Italy's combined health card and codice fiscale card — a plastic card that serves as both your national health service (SSN) enrollment card and your tax identification document. As a registered Italian resident, you receive it automatically by mail from the Agenzia delle Entrate within 2–6 weeks of completing your anagrafe registration. You do not need to apply separately. If it doesn't arrive, request it at any Agenzia delle Entrate office.

What Is the Tessera Sanitaria?

The tessera sanitaria (TS, or health card) is a physical card issued by the Agenzia delle Entrate that serves multiple purposes:

  1. Codice fiscale card: displays your 16-character Italian tax code
  2. SSN enrollment card: proves your enrollment in the Sistema Sanitario Nazionale (national health service)
  3. European Health Insurance Card (EHIC): for Italian residents, the tessera sanitaria doubles as the EHIC, which covers emergency healthcare across all EU/EEA countries

The card contains a microchip (since 2014) that can store your digital health data, though this functionality varies by region.

Who Gets a Tessera Sanitaria?

All Italian residents, including foreign nationals, are issued a tessera sanitaria. You will receive it if you are:

  • Registered at the Italian anagrafe (municipal population register)
  • An Italian citizen registered with AIRE (receives a codice fiscale-only version)
  • A non-resident assigned a codice fiscale by the Agenzia delle Entrate for tax purposes (may receive a TS without SSN benefits)

How You Receive Your Tessera Sanitaria

Automatic Issuance After Anagrafe Registration

For most Americans who complete their anagrafe registration, the tessera sanitaria arrives automatically:

  1. You complete anagrafe registration at your Comune
  2. The Comune transmits your data to the Agenzia delle Entrate
  3. The Agenzia delle Entrate generates your tessera sanitaria
  4. The card is mailed to your registered Italian address

Timeline: Typically 2–6 weeks after anagrafe registration completion.

💡 Tip

Make sure your registered address is one where you can reliably receive mail. Italian postal delivery can be inconsistent in some areas. If you are at a temporary address, arrange for someone to collect your mail.

If It Doesn't Arrive

If you have not received your tessera sanitaria within 6 weeks of completing anagrafe registration:

  1. Go to any Agenzia delle Entrate office
  2. Bring your passport and anagrafe registration certificate (certificato di residenza)
  3. Ask for a duplicate tessera sanitaria (duplicato tessera sanitaria)
  4. It will be issued on the spot or within a few days

What the Card Contains

The tessera sanitaria displays:

  • Your full name
  • Your codice fiscale (16-character alphanumeric code)
  • Your date of birth
  • The card's expiry date (usually 6 years from issuance)
  • Your ASL code (the local health authority code for your municipality)
  • TEAM data (Tessera Europea Assicurazione Malattia — for EU travel)
  • A microchip (CNS — Carta Nazionale dei Servizi)

The EHIC Function: Healthcare Across Europe

As an Italian resident holding a tessera sanitaria, you are automatically covered by the European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) system. The back of the tessera sanitaria functions as your EHIC.

What the EHIC covers:

  • Emergency and medically necessary treatment in any EU/EEA country
  • The same care level available to that country's own citizens
  • Doctor visits, hospital treatment, prescription medication at local resident rates

What the EHIC does NOT cover:

  • Private healthcare
  • Treatment you traveled specifically to receive
  • Repatriation to Italy or the USA
  • Non-emergency travel insurance
💡 Tip

When traveling within Europe, always carry your tessera sanitaria. Do not rely on a photocopy — the physical card is required in most countries.

Renewing Your Tessera Sanitaria

The tessera sanitaria expires (usually after 6 years). Renewal is automatic — the Agenzia delle Entrate mails a new card to your registered address before the old one expires. If you have moved, update your address at the anagrafe first, then request a new card.

The Tessera Sanitaria and the Medico di Base

Having a tessera sanitaria does not automatically enroll you with a specific doctor. To access Italy's national health service for primary care, you must separately enroll with a medico di base (general practitioner) through your local ASL (Azienda Sanitaria Locale).

How to Enroll With a Medico di Base

  1. Find your local ASL office (search "[your city] ASL" or "[your region] ASL")
  2. Bring your tessera sanitaria and permesso di soggiorno or carta d'identità
  3. Choose a medico di base from the ASL's list of available doctors in your area
  4. Complete the enrollment form
💡 Tip

In large cities, English-speaking medici di base exist and can be found on your ASL's doctor list. Ask the ASL office specifically for "medici che parlano inglese."

SSN Access for Americans: Who Qualifies?

StatusSSN Access
Registered resident with work incomeFull SSN access — mandatory enrollment
Registered resident without work income (e.g., elective residence)Can access SSN via voluntary enrollment — annual fee applies
Non-resident with codice fiscale onlyNo SSN access (codice fiscale-only version of TS)

Voluntary SSN Enrollment for Non-Working Residents

Americans on elective residence or digital nomad visas who do not have Italian employment income can enroll in the SSN voluntarily by paying an annual fee (contributo volontario). The fee is currently approximately €2,000/year for individuals (income-based — lower-income applicants may pay less). This gives you full SSN access — the same as an Italian employee.

This fee is significantly lower than the cost of private international health insurance (typically €3,000–€6,000/year for an American in their 50s–60s). Many Americans on elective residence visas choose voluntary SSN enrollment instead of private insurance. Application is made at your local ASL office.

What If You Lose Your Tessera Sanitaria?

Request a duplicate (duplicato) at:

  • Any Agenzia delle Entrate office (bring passport or carta d'identità)
  • Online via the Agenzia delle Entrate website (if you have SPID or CIE)
  • Your ASL office (for the health enrollment component)

The replacement card is free and typically issued within a few weeks.

The Digital Tessera Sanitaria

Since 2023, Italy has been rolling out a digital version accessible via the IO app (Italy's official government app) and the Fascicolo Sanitario Elettronico (digital health record). As of Q2 2025, the digital version is available in most regions for accessing health records, booking SSN appointments, and viewing prescriptions. The physical card remains the standard for most in-person interactions.

FAQ

My tessera sanitaria shows an incorrect name spelling — what do I do?
Request a correction (rettifica) at any Agenzia delle Entrate office, bringing your passport. The codice fiscale itself will not change, but the name on the card will be corrected.

Can I use my tessera sanitaria in the USA?
No. The EHIC only applies within EU/EEA countries. For US healthcare, you need separate US-compliant insurance if you visit.

I have a tessera sanitaria but my doctor doesn't accept it — what does that mean?
Private doctors do not accept the SSN card. The tessera sanitaria is for public (SSN) healthcare. Private consultations are paid out-of-pocket.

My permesso di soggiorno expired — does my tessera sanitaria still work?
As long as you have a valid ricevuta (permesso renewal pending), your SSN access continues. If your residency status lapses entirely, notify your ASL.
