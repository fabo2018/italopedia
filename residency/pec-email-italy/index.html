PEC Email in Italy: Why Every Resident Needs One and How to Get It

Italy's government does not communicate by regular email. It communicates by PEC — a certified email system that generates legally valid delivery receipts. If you live in Italy and you do not have a PEC address, you are effectively invisible to the Italian administrative system.

Most countries have moved government communications online via standard email. Italy moved online too — but it built its own parallel email infrastructure called PEC, and it is not compatible with Gmail, Outlook, or any standard email service.

PEC stands for Posta Elettronica Certificata (Certified Electronic Mail). It was established under the Codice dell'Amministrazione Digitale (D.Lgs. 82/2005) and is the only email format that Italian law treats as equivalent to registered mail (raccomandata). When a government agency, court, or official institution sends you a PEC message, it has the same legal weight as a physical certified letter with a return receipt.

What PEC Is and Why It Exists

Standard email has no proof of delivery. You can send an email to the Italian tax authority and they can deny receiving it, and you cannot prove them wrong. PEC solves this: the PEC system generates timestamped delivery receipts (ricevute di accettazione and ricevute di consegna) that are legally valid proof that a message was sent and received.

The mechanics: both sender and recipient must have PEC addresses. The PEC infrastructure is managed by certified providers (Gestori PEC) accredited by AGID (Agenzia per l'Italia Digitale). These providers run the authentication and receipt generation that standard email cannot provide.

Italy has required PEC for business communications since 2012. Individual residents are increasingly expected to have PEC — some procedures formally require it, others strongly assume it.

Why You Need One as a Foreign Resident

As an American living in Italy, you will need a PEC address for:

  • Communicating formally with the Agenzia delle Entrate (tax authority)
  • Receiving official communications from INPS (social security authority)
  • Court proceedings and legal notices (if any)
  • Some Italian banks require PEC for business accounts
  • Any Italian business registration or partita IVA requires a PEC address
  • Formal communications with your comune (municipality)
  • Some healthcare administrative processes

For residents who do not run a business and have simple bureaucratic needs, PEC remains useful rather than strictly mandatory. But at €5–€10/year, there is essentially no cost argument for not having one.

How to Open a PEC Account

Opening a PEC account is simpler than most Italian administrative processes:

  1. Choose a PEC provider (see comparison below)
  2. Go to their website and select a plan
  3. Provide your codice fiscale and a valid Italian ID (or passport for foreign residents)
  4. Complete identity verification — most providers now offer online verification via webcam or by uploading a document photo
  5. Pay the annual fee (€5–€25 depending on plan)
  6. Your PEC address is activated immediately or within hours

You do not need to be physically in Italy to open a PEC account, and you do not need a specific visa status. Any person who can provide identity verification can open a personal PEC account. However, if you are not yet registered in Italy with a codice fiscale, you will need to obtain one first — see our codice fiscale guide.

How to Use PEC Effectively

A few things about PEC that work differently from standard email:

  • You can only send PEC to PEC: A PEC address cannot send legally certified messages to a Gmail or Outlook address. For communications that require legal validity, both parties need PEC.
  • Check it regularly: Official Italian communications do not wait for you to check your email. Once a PEC message is delivered to your inbox, it is legally delivered — whether you read it or not. Miss a deadline in a PEC from the tax authority because you did not check it, and the deadline still passed.
  • Storage limits matter: If your PEC inbox is full, new messages cannot be delivered. On a basic 1 GB plan, keep it under 80% and archive messages regularly.
  • Your PEC address is permanent: Unlike standard email you might abandon, your PEC is tied to your legal identity. Keep it active and monitored as long as you are an Italian resident.

PEC and SPID: Italy's Two Digital Identity Tools

New arrivals often confuse PEC and SPID. They are different:

  • PEC: A certified email address for sending and receiving legal communications. Think of it as a digital registered mailbox.
  • SPID (Sistema Pubblico di Identità Digitale): A digital identity credential for logging into Italian government websites. Think of it as a government-issued username and password for online portals (INPS, Agenzia delle Entrate website, healthcare portals, municipal services).

You need both. SPID lets you access government portals. PEC lets you send and receive legally certified communications from those same entities. They are complementary, not redundant. See our separate guide on obtaining SPID as a foreign resident in Italy.

FAQ

Can a non-Italian resident open a PEC account?
Yes. PEC accounts are available to any individual who can provide identity documentation. Foreign residents with an Italian codice fiscale and a valid passport or Italian ID can open a PEC account from any of the major providers. Some providers have an Italian-only interface, but the process is manageable.
What happens if I miss a PEC message from the tax authority?
Legally, delivery is complete when the message reaches your PEC inbox — not when you open it. If the Agenzia delle Entrate sends you a notice with a response deadline, that deadline runs from delivery, not from when you read it. Monitor your PEC inbox at least weekly.
Do I need a PEC address to receive my permesso di soggiorno appointment?
The questura typically notifies permesso applicants by phone or SMS, not PEC. PEC is more relevant for tax, healthcare administrative, and business communications. That said, having PEC from day one of Italian residency is best practice.
