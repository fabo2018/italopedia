Getting a SIM Card in Italy as an American: The Complete 2025 Guide

Italy has some of Europe's cheapest mobile plans — and you need an Italian number for SPID, banking, and government offices. Here is how to get set up quickly. Updated 2025.

Quick Answer

Getting an Italian SIM card is straightforward and cheap — Italy has some of the most competitive mobile plans in Europe. You need your passport and codice fiscale to purchase a SIM. Plans start from €5–9/month with generous data allowances. Your US credit or debit card may be declined — bring cash as backup.

Why Get an Italian SIM?

As an American in Italy, an Italian SIM card is practically essential from day one:

  • SPID requires an Italian mobile number for OTP authentication — you cannot get SPID without one
  • Italian banks require an Italian number for account security codes
  • Questura and government offices contact you by Italian number for appointment reminders
  • Local calls are cheap or free within Italy with an Italian plan
  • Data costs are dramatically lower than roaming on a US plan

Even if you keep your US number active, you need an Italian number for Italian bureaucracy.

What You Need to Buy a SIM

Since 2023, Italian law requires:

  • Valid passport (original)
  • Codice fiscale (required since a 2023 regulatory update)
⚠ Important

Without a codice fiscale, you cannot legally purchase an Italian SIM. The SIM will be deactivated within days if activated without it. Get your codice fiscale first (5 minutes at any Agenzia delle Entrate or Italian consulate).

Where to Buy

Carrier Stores

Every city has TIM, Vodafone, and WindTre stores. Iliad has physical kiosks in major shopping centers. Staff in flagship stores often speak some English.

Electronics Stores

MediaWorld and Unieuro sell SIM cards for all major operators.

Tabacchi (Tobacco Shops)

Tabacchi sell SIM cards and top-up (ricarica) vouchers for most operators. Convenient, widespread, and open long hours. Less likely to have English-speaking staff.

Airport

SIM cards are available at Italian airports, but prices are often higher and plan selection is limited. Buy at a store in your destination city instead.

Prepaid vs. Contract Plans

Prepaid (prepagato / PAYG): No contract, no credit check. Top up as you go or set up automatic renewal. Most Americans start with prepaid. Monthly cost: €5–15 for excellent plans.

Contract (contratto / abbonamento): Monthly direct debit from Italian bank account. May require Italian ID card or permesso di soggiorno. For your first months in Italy, prepaid is simpler. Once you have an Italian bank account and permesso, you can switch if you prefer.

eSIM Options

Most iPhones (iPhone XS and later) and many Android phones support eSIM. TIM, Vodafone, WindTre, Iliad, and Fastweb all offer eSIM activation. eSIM is convenient — no physical SIM required, and you can keep your US SIM active simultaneously.

⚠ Important

Make sure your US phone is carrier-unlocked before arriving in Italy. Locked phones (still tied to AT&T, Verizon, T-Mobile, etc.) may not work with Italian SIMs. Contact your US carrier to unlock before departure.

Roaming in Italy With Your US Plan

For short visits or the first few days, roaming on your US plan is acceptable:

  • T-Mobile: Unlimited data (at 2G speeds) in Italy included on most plans
  • AT&T: International Day Pass (~$10/day)
  • Verizon: Travel Pass (~$10/day)
  • Google Fi: Works in Italy at domestic rates

For stays over 2 weeks, an Italian SIM is significantly cheaper.

Keeping Your Italian Number If You Leave

If you return to the USA temporarily, your Italian prepaid SIM will remain active as long as you top it up periodically. Most operators deactivate inactive prepaid SIMs after 12 months of no use. Top up with a small amount every 6–12 months to keep the number alive. Alternatively, switch to a contract plan — these remain active regardless of usage.

Common Problems

"My US card was declined at the carrier store."
Common. Italian POS terminals frequently reject foreign Visa/Mastercard cards without chip-and-PIN configuration. Bring cash (€10–20 is sufficient for a first SIM and top-up).

"The store says they can't activate without an Italian address."
Some stores require an address for contract SIMs. For prepaid, an address is typically not required. If pressed, give the address where you are staying temporarily.

"My SIM was deactivated."
Either your codice fiscale was not properly registered, or you did not top up within the required window. Go to the store with your passport and codice fiscale to reactivate.

FAQ

Can I keep my US number and have an Italian number?
Yes. Dual SIM phones allow both simultaneously. Most modern iPhones and Android phones support this.

Do Italian SIM cards work in other EU countries?
Yes. EU roaming regulations mean Italian SIMs include EU roaming in most plans. Some budget plans restrict this — check your plan's EU data allowance.

Can I transfer (port) my Italian number to a new operator?
Yes. Portability (portabilità del numero) is free and straightforward. Visit the new operator's store and request a port. The process takes 1–3 days.

Is there a minimum age to buy a SIM?
18 years old.
