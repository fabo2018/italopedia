SPID for Foreigners in Italy: How Americans Get Italy's Digital Identity (2025)

SPID is Italy's national digital identity system — a username and password that opens every Italian government website. As an American resident in Italy, you need it for taxes, healthcare, municipal services, and INPS. Here's how to get it.

Quick Answer

SPID (Sistema Pubblico di Identità Digitale) is Italy's national digital identity system — a single credential accepted by all Italian public administrations. As an American resident in Italy, you need SPID to access the Agenzia delle Entrate (tax office), INPS (social security), your municipality's online services, the Italian healthcare portal, and dozens of other platforms. You can get SPID if you have an Italian codice fiscale and a valid identity document. The process is free or low-cost and can be done entirely online with some providers.

Why You Need SPID

Since October 2021, SPID has replaced the old PIN-based systems for accessing Italian government websites. Without SPID, you cannot:

  • File or check your Italian tax return (730, Redditi)
  • Access your INPS pension and social security account
  • Apply for government benefits online
  • Book appointments at many public offices
  • Access your digital health records (Fascicolo Sanitario Elettronico)
  • Use many municipality online services (school enrollment, parking permits, etc.)
  • Access the AIRE portal (for Italian citizens abroad)

For Americans living in Italy, SPID is not optional — it is a practical necessity within the first few months of residency.

SPID Levels

LevelWhat It RequiresWhat It Unlocks
Level 1Username + passwordBasic services (rarely used)
Level 2Username + password + OTP (one-time code via app or SMS)All standard government services
Level 3Level 2 + hardware security deviceHigh-security services (rare)

For virtually all purposes, SPID Level 2 is what you need and what you will get when you register.

Who Can Get SPID?

You can apply for SPID if you have:

  • An Italian codice fiscale (mandatory)
  • A valid identity document: Italian carta d'identità, Italian permesso di soggiorno, or your US passport
  • An Italian mobile phone number (for OTP codes)
  • An email address
💡 Tip

Get your codice fiscale first. Without it, no SPID provider can proceed. Also get an Italian SIM card — foreign phone numbers are not accepted for OTP codes.

SPID Providers

SPID is issued by accredited private companies called Identity Providers (IdP), authorized by AgID (Agenzia per l'Italia Digitale). The main providers as of 2025:

How to Get SPID: Step-by-Step

Option A: In Person at Poste Italiane (Recommended for Beginners)

  1. Gather your documents: codice fiscale, valid identity document (permesso di soggiorno, carta d'identità, or US passport), Italian mobile number, email address
  2. Book an appointment at poste.it — select "Identità Digitale SPID" as the service
  3. Attend the appointment: the post office clerk will verify your identity and set up the PosteID app on your smartphone. Takes approximately 20–30 minutes
  4. Activate: follow the activation email, set your password, install the PosteID app. Your SPID Level 2 is now active

Cost: Free.

Option B: Online via Video Call (Aruba, InfoCert, or Namirial)

  1. Go to spid.gov.it and select your provider
  2. Fill in your codice fiscale, identity document details, Italian mobile number, and email
  3. Upload photos of your identity document (front and back)
  4. Schedule a video call with an operator — you will hold your document up to the camera (5–10 minutes)
  5. Follow the activation email, set your password, install the provider's app
💡 Tip

During the video call, the operator will ask you to show your document and your face simultaneously. Have good lighting and hold the document steady. If the call fails, you can reschedule.

Option C: Via CIE (Carta d'Identità Elettronica)

If you have an Italian carta d'identità elettronica (issued to registered residents), you can use it to get SPID or to access government services directly without SPID. The CIE has an NFC chip — use the CIE Identity App to authenticate.

Using SPID: Practical Guide

Once you have SPID, log in to any Italian government website by:

  1. Clicking "Accedi con SPID" on any government site
  2. Selecting your provider from the dropdown
  3. Entering your SPID username and password
  4. Approving the login on your smartphone app (OTP confirmation)

The most important services for American expats:

  • Agenzia delle Entrate: check tax position, file returns, request codice fiscale certificate
  • INPS: access contribution history and pension forecast (if you work in Italy)
  • Your Comune: book appointments, access certificates, pay fines
  • Fascicolo Sanitario Elettronico: access digital health records and prescriptions

SPID vs. CIE vs. CNS

SystemWhat It IsWho Has It
SPIDUsername/password + appAnyone with codice fiscale

SPID is the most universally accepted and the easiest to get for foreigners. For most Americans, SPID is the priority.

Common Problems

"I don't have an Italian mobile number yet." Get an Italian SIM card first. Foreign phone numbers are not accepted for OTP codes.

"My identity document was rejected." Ensure your documents are valid and photo uploads are clear. If your permesso di soggiorno is pending, some providers accept the postal ricevuta — check your specific provider's policy.

"The video call keeps failing." Common causes: poor lighting, unstable internet, document not readable on camera. Try a different time or switch to a provider with in-person verification.

FAQ

Does SPID expire? SPID itself does not expire, but you must log in at least once every 24 months or the account may be deactivated. Keep it active.

Can I have SPID from multiple providers? Yes. Each gives you a separate set of credentials, but they all access the same government services.

Is SPID safe? Yes. The two-factor authentication (password + app OTP) makes it significantly more secure than typical username/password logins.

What if I leave Italy — do I keep my SPID? Your SPID remains active as long as your identity documents are valid and you log in periodically.
