# Tessera Sanitaria in Italy: What It Is, How to Get One as an American Expat (2025)

The Tessera Sanitaria (TS) is one of the most important documents in Italian daily life — and one of the most confusing for Americans. It's Italy's national health card, combined with your Codice Fiscale (tax number) on a single physical card. You present it at every healthcare interaction. It's also increasingly used for electronic prescription management, pharmacies, and even some tax functions.

This guide explains exactly what the Tessera Sanitaria is, how Americans get one, what to do when it expires, and how it works with Italy's SSN healthcare system.

What Is the Tessera Sanitaria?

The Tessera Sanitaria is a card issued by the Agenzia delle Entrate (Italian Revenue Agency) that serves dual purposes:

  1. It IS your Codice Fiscale card — the 16-character alphanumeric Italian tax code is printed on it
  2. It IS your national health card — used for healthcare transactions within the SSN

What's on it:

  • Your full name
  • Date and place of birth
  • Codice Fiscale (tax code)
  • An electronic chip (on newer versions) for prescription management
  • Expiration date

What it looks like: A standard credit-card-sized plastic card, similar in format to a European health insurance card.

Legal basis: D.Lgs. 326/2003 (istituzione Tessera Sanitaria); Legge 27/2002 (Codice Fiscale integration)

Two Ways Americans Get a Tessera Sanitaria

The path to your Tessera Sanitaria depends on how you arrived in Italy and your relationship with the SSN.

Path 1: Via the Agenzia delle Entrate (Tax Number Only)

When you first arrive in Italy and get your Codice Fiscale from the Agenzia delle Entrate, you receive either:

  • A paper Codice Fiscale certificate (tesserino del codice fiscale), OR
  • The full Tessera Sanitaria card

Whether you get the full TS or just the paper depends on whether you're already linked to the SSN. In many cases, the Agenzia delle Entrate issues the full card proactively.

How to get your Codice Fiscale (and Tessera Sanitaria) from Agenzia delle Entrate:

  1. Go to any Agenzia delle Entrate office in Italy
  2. Request: "Vorrei richiedere il Codice Fiscale" (I'd like to request the Codice Fiscale)
  3. Bring: valid passport + any proof of address in Italy (rental contract, accommodation letter)
  4. Complete form AA4/8 (natural person Codice Fiscale application)
  5. Receive: either the card immediately or a paper certificate; full TS card may be issued by mail within weeks

Cost: Free

Processing: Immediate or within 30 days

Official reference: Form AA4/8 available at agenziaentrate.gov.it

Path 2: Via SSN Enrollment (Full Health Card)

When you enroll in Italy's SSN (national health service) at your local ASL (Azienda Sanitaria Locale), the ASL issues or activates your Tessera Sanitaria for healthcare use.

If you already have a Codice Fiscale card (from Path 1), the SSN enrollment links your health coverage to that same card number. You typically don't get a new physical card — the existing card becomes activated for healthcare.

The chip matters here: Newer Tessera Sanitaria cards have an electronic chip used for:

  • Receiving electronic prescriptions (Ricetta Elettronica)
  • Access to your digital health records (Fascicolo Sanitario Elettronico)
  • Pharmacist scanning for prescription dispensing

If your card doesn't have a chip, some healthcare functions require workarounds (your fiscal code entered manually), but the card still works for most purposes.

What You Use the Tessera Sanitaria For

At the Doctor (GP or Specialist)

Present your TS at every appointment. The doctor scans it or enters your Codice Fiscale to:

  • Access your health record
  • Issue electronic prescriptions
  • Record the visit in the SSN system

At the Pharmacy (Farmacia)

For SSN-covered prescriptions, the pharmacist:

  1. Scans your TS chip (or enters your Codice Fiscale)
  2. Retrieves your electronic prescription
  3. Dispenses the medication
  4. Charges you only the co-pay (ticket sanitario)

Without presenting your TS, you may have to pay full price for medications and claim reimbursement later — unnecessary hassle.

At the Hospital (Pronto Soccorso / Emergency Room)

Present at check-in. SSN coverage is verified immediately via the national system.

For Tax Deductions (730 Tax Return)

Italians can deduct medical expenses on their annual tax return (Modello 730). The Tessera Sanitaria is used to link medical expenditures to your tax file in the Sistema Tessera Sanitaria — Italy's centralized health expenditure tracking system.

Healthcare providers (doctors, pharmacies, hospitals) report expenses linked to your TS number to the tax authority, which pre-populates your tax deduction options in the 730 return.

Reference: D.Lgs. 68/2014 (Sistema Tessera Sanitaria for tax purposes)

For ISEE (Income/Wealth Declaration)

The TS chip can be used for digital identity verification in some ISEE application processes.

Tessera Sanitaria Europaea: The Travel Card

A separate but related document: the Tessera Europea di Assicurazione Malattia (TEAM) — also known as the European Health Insurance Card (EHIC).

This is printed on the back of your Tessera Sanitaria (or issued separately) and provides:

  • Coverage for medically necessary care in other EU/EEA countries
  • Same coverage as local citizens in those countries while traveling

As an American with Italian residency enrolled in the SSN: You qualify for the TEAM/EHIC. This means you can receive emergency healthcare across the EU under Italian SSN coverage — important for travel within Europe.

How to get your TEAM: Request it from your local ASL. It's typically printed on the back of newer TS cards or issued as a separate card if your TS doesn't have the TEAM section.

When the Tessera Sanitaria Expires

The Card Has an Expiration Date

Physical TS cards have an expiration date printed on them (typically 5–10 years for Italian citizens and EU residents; shorter for non-EU residents with temporary permits).

For Americans on a Permesso di Soggiorno, the TS expiration is often aligned with your Permesso validity — when your Permesso is renewed, you may need to renew your TS.

What Happens When It Expires

If your TS expires:

  • You cannot use the electronic chip for prescriptions
  • Some pharmacies may refuse to process SSN prescriptions
  • You should request renewal immediately

How to Renew

Renewal via Agenzia delle Entrate:

Go to any Agenzia delle Entrate office with:

  • Expired TS card
  • Valid Permesso di Soggiorno or EU documents
  • Passport

Request: "Voglio rinnovare la Tessera Sanitaria" (I want to renew my Tessera Sanitaria)

A new card is issued — sometimes immediately (paper tesserino first, physical card mailed within weeks).

Renewal via ASL:

If your SSN enrollment is being renewed simultaneously (you're renewing your voluntary SSN contribution), the ASL can initiate the TS renewal.

Online renewal (for some cases):

The Agenzia delle Entrate offers online renewal for Italian citizens. The process for non-citizens may still require an in-person visit — verify at agenziaentrate.gov.it.

Lost or Stolen Tessera Sanitaria

Report lost/stolen immediately to prevent identity theft (the TS card contains your tax identification number — useful to fraudsters).

Replace via:

  • Any Agenzia delle Entrate office (bring passport)
  • Some pharmacies (farmacie) are equipped to print a duplicate paper copy showing your Codice Fiscale — useful if you need to access healthcare before the replacement card arrives

Replacement is free.

Tessera Sanitaria vs. Codice Fiscale: The Difference

Many Americans are confused because they hear both terms. Clarity:

DocumentWhat It IsWho Issues
Codice Fiscale16-character alphanumeric tax identification numberAgenzia delle Entrate
Tessera del Codice FiscalePaper certificate showing your CFAgenzia delle Entrate
Tessera Sanitaria (TS)Physical card showing CF + health card functions + chipAgenzia delle Entrate + SSN data

Practical result: These are different names for the same physical card in most cases. The "Tessera Sanitaria" is the official name of the card that shows your Codice Fiscale and serves as your health card. When someone asks for your "Codice Fiscale," you show them this card.

Digital Tessera Sanitaria: The Future

Italy is progressively implementing digital health records and digital identity. The Fascicolo Sanitario Elettronico (FSE) — Electronic Health Record — is now being rolled out nationally, linked to your TS number.

Through the FSE (accessible via fascicolo-sanitario.gov.it with SPID or CIE digital identity):

  • View your electronic prescriptions
  • Access your medical history (with your doctors' inputs)
  • Manage consent for data sharing

To access FSE: You need either SPID (Italy's digital identity system) or CIE (Electronic Identity Card). Getting SPID is covered in our dedicated SPID guide.

Practical Tips

Get your Tessera Sanitaria on your first week in Italy. Along with Codice Fiscale, it's the document you'll need for everything — open a bank account, sign a lease, register at the Comune. Do not delay.

Always carry it. Italians carry their TS card routinely. Unlike the U.S. where you might carry a separate insurance card, in Italy the TS is the document for all healthcare and tax purposes.

Keep it safe but accessible. The Codice Fiscale on the card is your financial identity in Italy — treat it with the same care as your SSN card in the U.S.

Photograph both sides. Keep a digital copy in your phone and cloud storage. You'll need the number frequently (apartment contracts, bank accounts, medical forms) — having a photo means you don't need to carry the card every time.
