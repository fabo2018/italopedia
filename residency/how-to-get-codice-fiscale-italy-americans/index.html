How to Get a Codice Fiscale in Italy as an American: Complete 2025 Guide

A codice fiscale is Italy's universal identifier — 16 characters that unlock bank accounts, leases, SIM cards, healthcare, and every government form. Here's exactly how Americans get one, whether you're still in the US or already in Italy.

Quick Answer

A codice fiscale is Italy's tax identification code — a 16-character alphanumeric string assigned to every person with any legal or financial relationship with the Italian state. As an American, you need one before you can open a bank account, sign a lease, buy property, register for healthcare, get a SIM card, or apply for residency. You can get it at the nearest Italian consulate in the USA before you leave, or at any Agenzia delle Entrate office once you arrive in Italy. The process takes between 5 minutes (in person) and 4 weeks (by mail).

What Is a Codice Fiscale?

The codice fiscale is Italy's universal personal identifier — equivalent to a Social Security Number, but used far more broadly in daily life. It appears on your health card, every tax return, utility contracts, lease agreements, and virtually every official document you will ever sign in Italy.

The code is 16 characters long and is algorithmically derived from your surname, first name, date of birth, gender, and municipality of birth. It is permanent — it follows you for life and never changes.

The codice fiscale is not the same as a Partita IVA, which is the VAT number for self-employed people. You need a codice fiscale first; a Partita IVA is additional and only required if you work in Italy.

Who Needs a Codice Fiscale?

As an American planning to spend any meaningful time in Italy, you need a codice fiscale to:

  • Open an Italian bank account
  • Sign a rental contract (legally required on all leases)
  • Buy property in Italy
  • Register for the national health service (SSN)
  • Apply for a permesso di soggiorno
  • Register a SIM card (required since 2023)
  • Receive any income in Italy
  • File Italian taxes
  • Get married or receive an inheritance in Italy

Even if you are only visiting for 90 days and renting from a private landlord, you will almost certainly be asked for a codice fiscale. Get it before you arrive.

How to Get Your Codice Fiscale Before Leaving the USA

The easiest method for Americans is to apply at the Italian consulate covering your US state of residence. This is free, fast, and means you arrive in Italy already equipped.

Step 1: Find Your Consulate

Italy has consulates in New York, Los Angeles, Chicago, Houston, Miami, Boston, Philadelphia, San Francisco, Detroit, and several other cities. Each consulate has jurisdiction over specific US states. Check the full list at esteri.it.

Step 2: Gather Your Documents

  • Valid US passport (original + photocopy of the photo page)
  • Completed application form Modulo AA4/8 — downloadable from agenziaentrate.gov.it
  • For minors: birth certificate + parent's documents
💡 Tip

Important 2024 update: Since mid-2024, some Italian consulates in the US have applied stricter requirements. Check your specific consulate's current procedures before visiting — requirements vary and change.

Step 3: Submit

At most consulates, the codice fiscale is issued on the spot or within a few days. There is no fee. You receive a paper certificate (Attribuzione del Codice Fiscale) with your 16-character code.

How to Get Your Codice Fiscale Once in Italy

If you did not get your codice fiscale before leaving the USA, get it at any Agenzia delle Entrate office in Italy. There are offices in every provincial capital and most major towns.

Step 1: Find Your Local Office

Search for the nearest office at agenziaentrate.gov.it. Offices are typically open Monday–Friday, morning hours only (8:00–13:00 in most locations).

Step 2: Book an Appointment (or Walk In)

Since the pandemic, many offices have moved to appointment-only systems. Book online via the "Prenota un appuntamento" function, or call the national helpline at 800 909 696 (free from Italian landlines) or 0696668907 (from mobile/abroad).

Step 3: Bring Your Documents

  • Valid US passport (original)
  • Completed Modulo AA4/8 form
  • If you have an Italian address: the address where you are staying

Step 4: Receive Your Code

The officer will enter your details, calculate your code algorithmically, and print your certificate immediately. The entire appointment takes approximately 10–15 minutes.

💡 Tip

Go early. Even in smaller towns, Agenzia delle Entrate offices can have long waits. Arriving 15 minutes before opening often means being first in line.

How to Apply by Mail (From the USA)

If your consulate does not process requests in person, send:

  1. A completed and signed Modulo AA4/8
  2. A clear photocopy of your US passport (photo page)
  3. A stamped, self-addressed return envelope

Send to your jurisdictional Italian consulate. Processing time is typically 3–6 weeks.

Understanding Your Codice Fiscale

CharactersWhat They Represent
1–3First three consonants of your surname
4–6Consonants/vowels of your first name
7–8Last two digits of your birth year
9Letter representing your birth month
10–11Day of birth (+ 40 for women)
12–15Belfiore code of your municipality of birth
16Control character

For Americans born in the USA, characters 12–15 will start with Z (foreign country code) followed by the code for the United States: Z404.

The codice fiscale is free. There is no application fee at consulates, no fee at the Agenzia delle Entrate, and no annual fee.

The Tessera Sanitaria: Your Codice Fiscale Card

Once you become an Italian resident (registered at the anagrafe), the Agenzia delle Entrate will issue you a tessera sanitaria — a plastic card displaying your codice fiscale and health service information. This card serves as both your health card and your codice fiscale ID. Until then, your paper certificate is your proof.

Common Problems

"My name has special characters or is hyphenated." The algorithm normalizes these automatically. The officer will handle it correctly from your passport.

"I have a calculated code from an online tool — can I use it?" No. A calculated code is not officially valid. Banks, notaries, and government offices require the official certificate.

"I lost my certificate." Request a duplicate at any Agenzia delle Entrate office, free of charge, with your passport. Alternatively, verify and print online if you have an Italian SPID.

"Two people have the same code (omocodia)." The Agenzia delle Entrate resolves it automatically by substituting specific digits with letters. Rare but handled.

FAQ

Do I need a codice fiscale before arriving in Italy? No — you can get it after you arrive. Applying in person at an Agenzia delle Entrate is faster and more reliable than the consulate route.

Can I have more than one codice fiscale? No. Each person has exactly one, assigned for life.

Does it expire? No. It is permanent.

I am just buying a vacation home — do I need one? Yes. The notary (notaio) handling the property transfer is legally required to record your codice fiscale on the deed.

Can I get one for my child? Yes. Children can be assigned a codice fiscale via the same process; a parent or legal guardian must apply on behalf of minors.
