How to Get a Codice Fiscale in Italy as an American

Step-by-step guide for Americans on how to get an Italian codice fiscale (tax ID number) — in person, at the consulate, or online. Updated 2025.

If you're planning to move to Italy, rent an apartment, open a bank account, or access healthcare — the codice fiscale is the first thing you need. Without it, almost nothing works.

This guide covers exactly how Americans can get one, whether you're still in the US or already in Italy.

What Is the Codice Fiscale?

The codice fiscale is Italy's personal tax identification number, issued by the Agenzia delle Entrate (Italian Revenue Agency). It's a 16-character alphanumeric code calculated from your personal data: last name, first name, date of birth, gender, and place of birth.

You'll need it for:

  • Signing a rental contract
  • Opening an Italian bank account
  • Registering with the national health service (SSN)
  • Getting a SIM card
  • Starting a job
  • Buying property
  • Accessing any public service

Think of it as your Social Security Number — but for everything, not just taxes.

3 Ways to Get Your Codice Fiscale

Option 1: At an Agenzia delle Entrate Office in Italy (Fastest)

This is the most reliable method if you're already in Italy. It's free and usually done the same day.

What you need:

  • Valid US passport (minimum 6 months validity)
  • Visa or residence permit (if you have one)
  • Proof of address in Italy (rental contract, hotel booking — even informal proof helps)
  • Completed Form AA4/8 (download it from agenziaentrate.gov.it)

Steps:

  1. Download and fill out Form AA4/8 in advance
  2. Find your nearest Agenzia delle Entrate office
  3. Take a number and wait
  4. Submit your documents at the desk
  5. Receive your codice fiscale certificate on the spot or within a few days
💡 Tip

Fabrizio's note: I've been through this in Piedmont. Bring photocopies of everything — they'll ask for them. And go early in the morning.

Option 2: At the Italian Consulate in the US (Before You Move)

If you're still in the US and want your codice fiscale before arriving in Italy, you can apply through your local Italian consulate.

Important warning (updated 2025): Since July 2024, Italian consulates in the US have applied significantly stricter policies. Some now require original birth certificates and additional supporting documents. Check the specific requirements of your consulate before applying.

This route works but is slower and less predictable than applying in person in Italy.

Option 3: Online via Agenzia delle Entrate Portal (Limited)

The online codice fiscale application is currently only available to Italian citizens with a SPID digital identity or residents already registered in Italy. For most Americans arriving in Italy for the first time, in-person at the Agenzia delle Entrate is still the way to go.

What the Code Looks Like

Your codice fiscale will look something like this: BGGFRZ80A01L219K

It's calculated from your last name, first name, date of birth, gender, and place of birth. You cannot choose it — it's generated automatically from your data.

Common Problems Americans Face

"They asked for my residence permit and I don't have one yet." You don't need a residence permit to get a codice fiscale. Non-residents can apply. Some local branches apply rules inconsistently — calmly explain you are applying as a non-resident.

"The consulate wants documents I don't have." Consulate requirements have become stricter since 2024. If the consulate route is too complicated, wait until you arrive in Italy and apply in person — it's much faster.

"I lost my codice fiscale certificate." You can request a duplicate at any Agenzia delle Entrate office. The code itself never changes.

FAQ

Do I need a codice fiscale before arriving in Italy? No — you can get it after you arrive. In fact, applying in person at an Agenzia delle Entrate office in Italy is faster and more reliable than the consulate route.

Is the codice fiscale free? Yes. Applying directly at Agenzia delle Entrate is completely free.

Does my codice fiscale expire? No. Once issued, it's yours for life and never expires.

Do I need a codice fiscale to buy property in Italy? Yes. It's required for any real estate transaction in Italy, including purchases by non-residents.
