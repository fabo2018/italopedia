Marca da Bollo: The €16 Italian Government Stamp, Fully Explained

You will encounter the marca da bollo on your first trip to a government office in Italy. It is a €16 stamp that must be affixed to certain official documents — and if you attach the wrong type, submit an expired one, or put it in the wrong place, your document gets rejected. Here is everything you need to know.

Italy has a centuries-old system of stamping official documents to indicate a fee has been paid — a system formalized today as the imposta di bollo (stamp duty). The physical token of this payment is the marca da bollo: a sticker or paper stamp, currently valued at €16, that you affix to official documents before submitting them to government offices.

The concept has no direct American equivalent. In the US, government fees are paid at the counter or online. In Italy, you pre-purchase a mark of payment at a tabaccheria (tobacco shop) and physically attach it to the document. Submit the document without it and it is returned or rejected — no exceptions.

What a Marca da Bollo Is

The marca da bollo is a self-adhesive stamp sold at authorized vendors (primarily tabaccherie and some post offices) that represents payment of the Italian stamp duty (imposta di bollo). The current standard value is €16.00. It carries a unique serial number, a date code, and an Agenzia delle Entrate verification mark.

It is issued by the Agenzia delle Entrate and distributed through the tabaccherie network under license. You cannot buy them directly from the Agenzia or most government offices — you go to a tabacchi.

When You Need One

The imposta di bollo applies to a wide range of official documents. As a resident expat, you will most commonly need marche da bollo for:

  • Permesso di soggiorno applications: The kit postale typically requires 1–2 marche da bollo
  • Petitions and requests to government offices: Most written petitions (istanze) to the questura, anagrafe, or other public offices require 1 marca
  • Powers of attorney (procure): Notarized powers of attorney require marche da bollo
  • Lease contracts: Rental contracts subject to registration require marche da bollo (typically 1 per page of the contract above a threshold value)
  • Certified translations: Sworn translations submitted to government offices often require a marca da bollo
  • Bank account documentation: Some bank account openings and certain financial documents
  • Codice fiscale requests: Some formal requests to the Agenzia delle Entrate

When in doubt about whether a document requires a marca da bollo, ask at the office before submitting. They will tell you — this is a routine question and the answer is definitive for each document type.

Where to Buy One

Any tabaccheria (tobacco shop, identified by the black T sign with white lettering) sells marche da bollo. Most tabaccherie are open 7:30am–12:30pm and 3:30–7:30pm, Monday–Saturday. Some are open longer hours.

What to say: "Una marca da bollo da sedici euro" (one sixteen-euro revenue stamp). The tobacconist will print one for you from their electronic terminal — modern marche da bollo are computer-generated, not pre-printed stock.

They can also be purchased at some post offices (Poste Italiane) and, increasingly, through digital channels for documents submitted electronically.

How to Use It Correctly

Placement rules:

  • Affix the marca da bollo in the upper-right area of the first page of the document (or as instructed by the specific form's instructions)
  • Do not cover any text or signatures with the marca
  • Do not fold, cut, or damage the marca
  • The marca must be affixed before the document is signed and dated — it should be part of the original document, not added after the fact
  • For multi-page documents: some require one marca per 4 pages; check the specific rules for your document type

The Expiration Date Trap

This is the most common mistake. Marche da bollo carry an expiration date — typically printed as "utilizzare entro" (use by) on the stamp. The standard validity is approximately 60 days from purchase.

If you submit a document with an expired marca da bollo, the receiving office will reject it. You will need to buy a new marca and resubmit. There is no grace period and no waiver for expired marche.

Practical rule: Buy marche da bollo only when you are ready to use them within the next few weeks. Do not buy a stock of them "for later" — they expire.

Digital Marca da Bollo

Italy has been phasing in a digital equivalent — the contrassegno telematico — for documents submitted electronically through official portals. If you file documents through the Agenzia delle Entrate online portal, the F23 or F24 payment form replaces the physical marca da bollo for most tax-related submissions.

For physical documents submitted in person at government offices: the physical marca da bollo remains required. The digital version is only valid for electronic submissions through the official digital channels.

Annual Cost for a Typical Expat

A new American resident in Italy typically uses 8–15 marche da bollo in their first year — permesso application, anagrafe registration, lease registration, various petitions and requests. At €16 each, this is €128–€240 in the first year. Subsequent years typically involve fewer — perhaps 4–8 — as you have fewer new applications and more renewals with familiar procedures.

The marca da bollo is the most visible face of Italy's imposta di bollo system, but the broader tax applies to many financial documents as well — bank account statements, insurance policies, and investment account documents may carry imposta di bollo charges that appear on your statements rather than requiring you to physically purchase a stamp.

FAQ

What if I don't have a marca da bollo with me when I arrive at a government office?
In most cases, you will be sent to buy one before your document can be accepted. Many government offices are near tabaccherie precisely for this reason. Allow extra time when visiting any Italian government office — buying a marca da bollo and returning to the queue is a routine part of the process. Arriving prepared is always better.
Can I get a refund on an unused marca da bollo?
Generally no. Marche da bollo are not refundable. If you purchase one and do not use it before it expires, the €16 is lost. This is one reason to buy them only when immediately needed.
My document requires a marca da bollo but I submitted without one. Can I add it afterward?
No. The marca da bollo must be part of the original document before submission. Adding it afterward and presenting it as the original document submitted is not permitted and could constitute document irregularity. Resubmit the correct document with the marca properly affixed.
