IRPEF Tax Brackets Italy 2025: How Italy's Income Tax Works for Americans

Italy's personal income tax (IRPEF) has three brackets for 2025: 23%, 35%, and 43%. For most Americans living in Italy, IRPEF applies to worldwide income — but several special regimes can replace it entirely.

Quick Answer

IRPEF (Imposta sul Reddito delle Persone Fisiche) is Italy's personal income tax. For 2025, Italy has three brackets: 23% on income up to €28,000; 35% on income between €28,001 and €50,000; 43% on income above €50,000. These rates apply to Italian-source income and, for Italian tax residents, worldwide income — unless you have elected a special regime (€100k flat tax, 7% pensioner regime, or impatriati regime). Americans living in Italy need to understand both IRPEF and their continuing US tax obligations.

What Is IRPEF?

IRPEF (Imposta sul Reddito delle Persone Fisiche) is the Italian personal income tax. It is levied on:

  • Italian tax residents: worldwide income (from any source, Italian or foreign)
  • Non-residents: only income produced in Italy

Most Americans living in Italy become Italian tax residents (by spending 183+ days/year in Italy or being registered in the anagrafe), which means IRPEF applies to their worldwide income — including US Social Security, US investment income, and US rental income.

2025 IRPEF Brackets

Following reforms introduced by Legge di Bilancio 2024, Italy's IRPEF structure for 2025 is:

Income RangeRate
€0 – €28,00023%
€28,001 – €50,00035%
Over €50,00043%

How the brackets work (marginal rates): If you earn €60,000:

  • First €28,000 × 23% = €6,440
  • Next €22,000 (€28,001–€50,000) × 35% = €7,700
  • Remaining €10,000 × 43% = €4,300
  • Total IRPEF: €18,440 (effective rate: ~30.7%)

Additional Taxes on Top of IRPEF

Regional Income Tax (Addizionale Regionale)

Charged by the region where you are registered. Typical range: 1.23% – 3.33%.

  • Piedmont: 1.23% (one of the lowest)
  • Lazio (Rome): up to 3.33% (one of the highest)

Municipal Income Tax (Addizionale Comunale)

Charged by your municipality. Range: 0% – 0.9%. Many small municipalities: 0.2% – 0.5%.

Combined Tax Burden Example

For an Italian resident in Turin (Piedmont) earning €50,000:

  • IRPEF: ~€14,140
  • Regional tax (Piedmont 1.23%): €615
  • Municipal tax (Turin ~0.8%): €400
  • Total: ~€15,155 (effective rate: ~30.3%)

Deductions and Tax Credits

Key deductions: Social security contributions (INPS), alimony payments, charitable donations to qualifying Italian nonprofits, medical expenses exceeding €129.11 (19% deduction), mortgage interest for primary residence.

Key credits: Employment income deduction (detrazione per lavoro dipendente), pension income deduction (for lower-income pensioners), family deductions for dependent children.

IRPEF and Americans: The Double Taxation Problem

Americans living in Italy face taxation from two directions:

  1. Italy: taxes worldwide income under IRPEF (as Italian residents)
  2. USA: taxes worldwide income under US federal income tax (as US citizens, regardless of residence)

The solution: The US-Italy Tax Treaty and Foreign Tax Credit. The US-Italy Tax Treaty allocates taxing rights between the two countries for specific income types. The US Foreign Tax Credit (Form 1116) allows you to credit Italian taxes paid against your US tax liability.

In practice for most Americans: Italian IRPEF rates are often higher than US rates on the same income → US FTC usually covers your entire US tax liability on that income → you typically end up paying Italian rates (not paying twice). However, the interaction is complex — work with a dual-qualified US-Italian tax professional.

Special Regimes That Replace IRPEF

1. Flat Tax Regime (Art. 24-bis) — €100,000/year

Available to new Italian residents (not resident for 9 of last 10 years) with any level of foreign income. Pay €100,000 flat regardless of how much you earn abroad. All Italian-source income taxed normally.

Best for: High-net-worth Americans with significant foreign investment income.

2. Southern Italy 7% Pensioner Regime (Art. 24-ter)

Pay 7% on all foreign income for 10 years if you move to a qualifying southern Italian municipality under 20,000 inhabitants.

Best for: American retirees with modest to moderate foreign pensions.

3. Impatriati Regime (Rientro dei Cervelli)

50% of employment or self-employment income exempt from IRPEF for 5 years for workers who transfer their tax residency to Italy.

Best for: Americans who are employed or self-employed and moving to Italy to work.

4. Forfettario Regime

For self-employed people with revenue under €85,000/year. Pay a flat 15% (or 5% for the first 5 years of business) on a forfeit percentage of revenues instead of IRPEF.

Best for: Freelancers and small business owners.

Frequently Asked Questions

I'm retired in Italy and my only income is US Social Security — do I pay IRPEF on it?
Under the US-Italy treaty, US Social Security is taxable only in the USA, not in Italy. It is generally excluded from IRPEF. Consult a tax professional for your specific situation.

I have US investment income — how is it taxed in Italy?
Dividends and capital gains from foreign investments are typically taxed in Italy at a flat 26% rate (separate from IRPEF), unless you have elected the €100,000 flat tax regime.

Do I need to hire an Italian accountant (commercialista)?
For Americans with US investment income, rental income, or business income, a commercialista experienced with US-Italian taxation is strongly recommended.

My IRPEF bracket is 43% — should I elect the flat tax?
The €100,000 flat tax covers only foreign-source income. If your total foreign income is above approximately €235,000/year, the flat tax saves money. Below that level, it likely costs more.
