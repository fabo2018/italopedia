Italian Tax Year Calendar 2025: Every Deadline Americans Care About

Italy's tax year runs January–December, but the payment schedule is backloaded in ways that surprise Americans. The big IRPEF payment hits in November. IMU property tax comes in June and December. Here is the full calendar with amounts and what happens if you miss a deadline.

The Italian tax year runs January 1 through December 31 — same as the US. But Italy's payment schedule is structured differently from what Americans expect: you pay income tax in arrears (the prior year's income tax, paid the following year), plus advance payments (acconti) toward the current year, all concentrated in two big payment windows in June and November.

Miss an Italian tax deadline and the penalty starts immediately — ravvedimento operoso (voluntary correction) allows you to pay with a reduced penalty, but only if you act before the Agenzia delle Entrate notices.

Italian Tax Year Overview

  • Tax year: January 1 – December 31
  • Tax return filing: April–November of the following year (varies by form)
  • Main payment windows: June (saldo + first acconto) and November (second acconto)
  • Property tax (IMU): June 16 and December 16
  • Fiscal year vs calendar year: Italy uses calendar years — no different fiscal year elections for individuals

IRPEF: Acconto and Saldo Explained

Italy's income tax payment system has two components that confuse first-year residents:

Saldo (balance): The final payment of last year's income tax. Filed and paid when you submit your Modello Redditi (by October 31 for the prior year). This is the actual tax owed for the previous calendar year, minus any withholding already paid.

Acconto (advance payment): An advance payment toward the current year's expected tax liability, based on the previous year's tax. Italy requires two acconto installments: a first installment in June (40% of the advance) and a second, larger installment in November (60% of the advance).

The November payment is the one that catches Americans off guard. If you had significant Italian income in 2024, your November 2025 acconto payment could be substantial — it is based on 100% of your 2024 Italian tax liability. Plan for this cash outflow in autumn.

You can reduce the acconto payment if you can demonstrate that your 2025 income will be lower than 2024 — this requires a deliberate election and carries some risk if income turns out higher than projected (the shortfall triggers a penalty).

IMU Property Tax Deadlines

IMU (Imposta Municipale Propria) is Italy's property tax, levied by municipalities on real estate. Deadlines:

  • June 16: First IMU installment — 50% of the prior year's total IMU payment, or a calculated advance if you are a first-year owner
  • December 16: Final IMU balance — the remainder after the June payment, calculated on the actual current-year IMU amount

Primary residence (prima casa) is exempt from IMU for non-luxury properties. Americans who own Italian property but live elsewhere (renting or owning a separate primary residence) pay IMU on their Italian property even if it is their only Italian asset. IMU rates vary by municipality — typically 0.86–1.14% of the property's cadastral value (not market value).

Late Payment Penalties

Italy's late payment penalty system:

  • 1 day late: 0.1% penalty per day for the first 14 days
  • 15–30 days late: 1.5% flat penalty
  • 31–90 days late: 1.67% flat penalty
  • 91 days to 1 year: 3.75% flat penalty
  • Over 1 year: 5% penalty, plus annual interest of 2.5%
  • Post-assessment (after Agenzia delle Entrate notices you): 25% base penalty, negotiable through accertamento procedures

The ravvedimento operoso mechanism allows voluntary late payment with the reduced penalties above — but only before the Agenzia has formally initiated a check. Pay late voluntarily and you pay a small percentage. Wait until they find you and the penalty jumps to 25%+.

FAQ

Can I file my Italian tax return in English?
No. All Italian tax returns must be filed in Italian through the Agenzia delle Entrate's systems. Modello Redditi PF is completed in Italian. Your commercialista handles this — they prepare and submit the return on your behalf through their professional filing credentials.
What is the 730 vs Modello Redditi — which do I use?
The 730 (precompilato) is a simplified return available to employees and pensioners with relatively straightforward Italian income. The Modello Redditi PF is the comprehensive return used by anyone with self-employment income, foreign income, investments, or complex situations — which includes virtually all American residents. Your commercialista will use Modello Redditi.
Does Italy tax its residents on a cash or accrual basis?
Italian personal income tax generally uses the cash basis for individuals — income is recognized when received, not when earned. Capital gains are recognized on the sale date. Rental income is recognized when collected. Your commercialista will confirm the specifics for each income category in your situation.
