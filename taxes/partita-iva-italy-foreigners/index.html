Partita IVA Italy for Americans: How to Get a VAT Number as a Foreigner (2025)

Complete guide to opening a Partita IVA in Italy as an American expat — eligibility, tax regimes (Forfettario vs. Ordinario), the registration process, and what ongoing compliance looks like.

To work self-employed in Italy you need a Partita IVA — Italy's 11-digit business tax number. Requirements: a visa that permits work (Permesso Familiare, Digital Nomad Visa, or Lavoro Autonomo), a Codice Fiscale, and Italian residency. If you want to work for yourself in Italy — as a freelancer, consultant, independent contractor, or small business owner — you need a Partita IVA. It's Italy's equivalent of a business tax identification number and the legal mechanism by which self-employed people bill clients and pay taxes.

For Americans in Italy, the Partita IVA opens up a straightforward path to self-employment, particularly with the Regime Forfettario (flat-rate tax scheme) that keeps taxes and administration remarkably simple for new businesses under €85,000/year in revenue.

I opened my own Partita IVA as a foreigner in Italy. Here's what you need to know.

What Is a Partita IVA?

A Partita IVA (literally "VAT number") is an 11-digit tax registration number issued by the Agenzia delle Entrate (Italian Revenue Agency) that identifies you as a self-employed professional or business entity.

Once you have one, you can: - Issue invoices to clients in Italy and abroad - Sign contracts as a self-employed professional - Open a dedicated business bank account - Participate in Italy's pension system as self-employed (INPS Gestione Separata) - File Italian taxes as a self-employed worker

Important distinction: A Partita IVA is a tax registration — not a business license. Regulated professions (doctors, lawyers, architects) require separate professional registration with their respective ordine professionale (professional association). Consult with a commercialista if you work in a regulated field.

Can Americans Get a Partita IVA?

Yes, with the right visa. You must have legal right to work in Italy. This depends on your visa status:

Visa / Status Can Open Partita IVA?
Permesso di Soggiorno per Lavoro Autonomo ✅ Yes
Permesso per Motivi Familiari ✅ Yes
Permesso per Soggiornante di Lungo Periodo ✅ Yes
EU Citizen (or family member) ✅ Yes
Digital Nomad Visa ✅ Yes (foreign clients only)
Elective Residency Visa ❌ No (work not permitted)
Tourist / Schengen Stay ❌ No

You also need: - An Italian Codice Fiscale (tax number) — obtained free from the Agenzia delle Entrate - Italian residency (Residenza) — registered at your Comune

Note: You can start the Partita IVA process as soon as you have your Codice Fiscale and the right visa, even before your Permesso is finalized. The Agenzia delle Entrate issues the number, but you need the right to work before you actually bill clients.

Choosing Your Tax Regime: The Key Decision

Italy has two main tax regimes for self-employed individuals. This is the most consequential decision you'll make when opening your Partita IVA.

Option 1: Regime Forfettario (Flat-Rate Scheme)

This is the default and recommended option for most American freelancers and consultants in Italy.

How it works: - You apply a fixed percentage (the "coefficient") to your gross revenue to calculate taxable income - You pay 15% tax on that taxable income (reduced to 5% for the first 5 years of new activity) - No VAT charged or collected — you don't charge IVA on your invoices (massive simplification) - No bookkeeping required — you track only revenue, not expenses

Eligibility requirements: - Annual revenue under €85,000 (threshold as of 2023 budget law) - No employees or collaborators with significant labor costs - No controlling interest in Italian companies in the same field - Not already working as employee in the same field (with some exceptions) - Not applicable if you have revenues from employment income exceeding €30,000

Coefficients by activity category (examples):

Activity Coefficient Effective Tax Rate (5yr) Effective Tax Rate (standard)
Professional / intellectual services 78% 3.9% of revenue 11.7% of revenue
Commerce (general) 40% 2% of revenue 6% of revenue
Craft trades 67% 3.35% of revenue 10.05% of revenue
Catering 40% 2% of revenue 6% of revenue

Example: American consultant earning €60,000/year in Italy - Taxable income = €60,000 × 78% = €46,800 - Tax (first 5 years) = €46,800 × 5% = €2,340/year - INPS contribution (Gestione Separata, 2025 rate ~26.07%) = €46,800 × 26.07% = ~€12,200/year - Total fiscal/social burden: ~€14,500/year on €60,000 revenue

This is remarkably favorable compared to Italian standard tax rates (43%+ on equivalent income as an employee).

Official reference: L. 190/2014 (Legge di Stabilità 2015); L. 197/2022 (modifications to threshold)

Option 2: Regime Ordinario (Standard Accounting)

The standard regime applies by default when you exceed €85,000 revenue or choose to opt out of Forfettario.

Key differences: - Full IRPEF rates apply (23%–43% progressive on taxable income) - VAT collected and remitted — you charge 22% IVA on invoices, submit quarterly/annual returns - Full bookkeeping required — income and expenses tracked (expenses deductible) - Requires a commercialista to manage — compliance is substantially more complex

For most Americans starting freelance work in Italy under €85,000/year, the Forfettario is clearly superior. Switch to Ordinario only when revenue justifies it or circumstances require.

How to Open a Partita IVA: Step by Step

Method 1: Self-Registration (Free)

Time: 1–2 hours Cost: Free

  1. Obtain your Codice Fiscale if you don't have one already (from Agenzia delle Entrate or Italian consulate)

  2. Download Form AA9/12 — the official application form for natural persons (individuals, not companies)

  3. Available at agenziaentrate.gov.it under "Modulistica"

  4. Fill out Form AA9/12:

  5. Your Codice Fiscale
  6. Your activity description
  7. ATECO code (see below)
  8. Tax regime selection (Forfettario recommended)

  9. Effective start date

  10. Submit the form — three options:

  11. In person at your local Agenzia delle Entrate office (bring ID + Codice Fiscale)
  12. By mail (raccomandata to your competent Agenzia delle Entrate office)

  13. Online via the Fisconline/Entratel portal (requires SPID or CNS digital identity)

  14. Receive your Partita IVA — issued immediately in person; within a few days by mail/online

Method 2: Through a Commercialista (Recommended)

Time: 1–2 weeks Cost: €100–300 one-time setup fee

A commercialista handles the registration and ensures you: - Choose the correct ATECO code - Register correctly with INPS (pension) - Set up your invoicing system - Understand your quarterly/annual obligations

Strongly recommended if you're unfamiliar with Italian tax law, have complex income sources, or are choosing between U.S. and Italian tax structures.

ATECO Code: What It Is and Why It Matters

The ATECO code is Italy's business activity classification system (equivalent to U.S. NAICS codes). Every Partita IVA holder must declare an ATECO code that describes their work.

The ATECO code determines your coefficient in the Forfettario regime and affects which INPS fund you contribute to.

Common ATECO codes for American expats:

Activity ATECO Code
IT consultant / software developer 62.01.09 or 62.02.00
Marketing consultant 73.11.02
Copywriter / translator 74.30.00
English teacher (private lessons) 85.59.20
Financial consultant 66.19.09
Business consultant 70.22.09
Photographer 74.20.19
Designer (graphic, web) 74.10.21

Find the complete list at ateco.istat.it or ask a commercialista to confirm the right code for your specific activity.

INPS Registration: The Pension Obligation

When you open a Partita IVA, you automatically become subject to INPS (National Social Security) contributions. The contribution fund depends on your ATECO code:

  • Gestione Separata (Separate Management): For most intellectual/professional services. Rate: ~26.07% of taxable income (2025). Applied to the net income calculated under Forfettario.
  • Artigiani e Commercianti: For craft and trade activities. Different rate structure.
  • Casse Professionali: If you're in a regulated profession (lawyer, accountant, engineer, etc.), you contribute to that profession's private fund instead of INPS.

For the Forfettario example above (€60,000 revenue, intellectual services): INPS Gestione Separata ~€12,200/year is the main social cost to account for.

These INPS contributions build your Italian pension entitlement. As an American, you can receive both U.S. Social Security and Italian INPS pension — there's no double-claiming problem between the two systems.

Annual Obligations with a Partita IVA

Invoicing

In Regime Forfettario, your invoices must: - Show your name, address, Codice Fiscale, and Partita IVA - Show the client's details - State "Operazione in franchigia IVA ai sensi dell'art. 1, commi da 54 a 89, legge n. 190/2014" (standard Forfettario IVA exemption language) - Include a €2 marca da bollo (revenue stamp) on each invoice over €77.47 (you either apply a physical stamp or pay electronically via F24)

Electronic Invoicing (Fattura Elettronica)

Since January 2024, electronic invoicing is mandatory for all Partita IVA holders, including Forfettario. You must issue invoices through the SDI (Sistema di Interscambio) — Italy's national electronic invoice exchange.

Use compliant invoicing software: - Aruba (Italian, affordable) - Fatture in Cloud (popular with commercialisti) - Invoiceberry or FreshBooks (if they support SDI integration) - Many banks include e-invoicing tools in business accounts

Annual Tax Return

File the Modello Redditi PF (personal income tax return) annually by November 30. Your commercialista handles this, or you can use the CAF (tax assistance center) service.

INPS Payments

Paid in two installments: June and November, using the Modello F24 payment form (pays taxes and social security in one transaction).

U.S. Tax Implications

Self-Employment Tax (SE Tax)

As a U.S. citizen working self-employed abroad, you still owe U.S. Self-Employment Tax (15.3%) on your net self-employment income unless covered by the U.S.-Italy Totalization Agreement.

The Totalization Agreement (in force since 1978) prevents double social security contributions. If you're paying Italian INPS contributions, you should be exempt from U.S. SE tax. This requires Form SSA-2490 to obtain a Certificate of Coverage from SSA.

Reference: U.S.-Italy Social Security Agreement; SSA Publication No. 05-10061

Foreign Earned Income Exclusion

Income from Italian Partita IVA work qualifies for the FEIE (Form 2555) — excluding up to $126,500 (2024) from U.S. taxable income if you meet the bona fide residence or physical presence test.

Report Partita IVA Income to IRS

You still report your Italian self-employment income on your U.S. 1040, Schedule SE, and potentially the FEIE Form 2555. Get a U.S. accountant familiar with expat taxation.

Practical Tips

Open your Partita IVA before you land your first Italian client. You cannot bill legally without it, and opening it takes 1–10 business days. Don't lose revenue waiting on paperwork.

Use a commercialista for the first year. The annual cost (€500–1,500/year for Forfettario compliance) pays for itself in avoided mistakes. After one full cycle, many people handle routine compliance themselves.

Keep all your invoices and receipts organized. Even in Forfettario (where expenses aren't deductible), you'll need them for cash flow records and any audit.

Invoice in EUR, not USD. The Partita IVA is an Italian tax instrument — invoice in euros. If your clients pay in USD, the exchange rate difference is your profit/loss, not an Italy tax issue.
