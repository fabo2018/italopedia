US-Italy Social Security Totalization Agreement: What Americans Need to Know

How the US-Italy Social Security Totalization Agreement works, who benefits from it, how to combine work credits from both countries, and what the 2025 Fairness Act changes for Americans in Italy.

If you've worked in both the United States and Italy, you might be paying social security taxes twice — or missing out on benefits you've earned. The US-Italy Social Security Totalization Agreement prevents the first problem and solves the second.

What Is the Totalization Agreement?

The United States and Italy signed a bilateral Social Security Totalization Agreement that has been in force since 1978. It does two things:

1. Prevents dual Social Security taxation Without the agreement, Americans working in Italy could be required to pay Social Security contributions to both the US (SSA) and Italy (INPS) simultaneously. The agreement prevents this duplication.

2. Allows combining of work credits If you haven't worked long enough in either country alone to qualify for retirement benefits, you can combine your work credits from both countries to meet the minimum requirements.

Preventing Double Taxation: Who Pays What

For Americans Working in Italy

If you work for an Italian employer in Italy (employed or self-employed), you generally pay Italian social security (INPS) and are exempt from US Social Security taxes on that same income.

For Americans Working Remotely for US Employers from Italy

This is a gray area. Remote workers employed by US companies but physically located in Italy may have complex dual obligations. A cross-border tax advisor is essential.

Combining Work Credits: The Most Important Benefit for Retirees

How US Social Security Credits Work

You need 40 quarters (10 years) of US Social Security contributions to qualify for US retirement benefits.

The Totalization Solution

Example: You worked 6 years in the US (24 credits — not enough) and 8 years in Italy. Under totalization, the US can consider your Italian credits to determine if you qualify. Each country then pays a proportional benefit based on the credits you actually earned there.

Important limits:

  • US can only count Italian credits if you have at least 6 US quarters (1.5 years) of US credits
  • Italy can only count US credits if you have at least 52 weeks of Italian contributions

The 2025 Social Security Fairness Act: What Changed

In January 2025, the Social Security Fairness Act was signed into law in the US, repealing two provisions:

  • Windfall Elimination Provision (WEP): Previously reduced US Social Security benefits for people who also received a pension from work not covered by US Social Security (including Italian INPS pension). Now repealed.
  • Government Pension Offset (GPO): Previously reduced spousal and survivor benefits. Now repealed.

What this means for Americans in Italy: If you receive both US Social Security and Italian INPS pension, you can now receive your full US Social Security benefit without reduction. If you were previously subject to WEP or GPO reductions, you may be eligible for a retroactive payment.

How to Apply for Totalization Benefits

For US Social Security Benefits Using Italian Credits

Contact the US Social Security Administration at your local SSA office, or contact the US Embassy in Rome which handles SSA inquiries for Americans in Italy.

For Italian Pension (INPS) Benefits Using US Credits

Contact INPS (Istituto Nazionale della Previdenza Sociale) at your local INPS office in Italy with your codice fiscale and documentation of your US Social Security work history.

FAQ

Does the Totalization Agreement mean I don't have to pay US taxes in Italy? No. The Totalization Agreement only covers Social Security contributions and benefits — not income taxes. Americans living in Italy still file US federal income tax returns annually.

Can I receive both a US Social Security benefit and an Italian INPS pension? Yes, if you've earned benefits in both systems. Since the repeal of WEP in 2025, you receive your full US benefit without reduction.

I worked in Italy in the 1990s — do those years count? Yes, if you paid INPS contributions in Italy, those years may count toward totalization. The agreement covers Italian credits earned after 1936.
