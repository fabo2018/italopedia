Italy Flat Tax for Foreigners: The Complete Guide for Americans (2025–2026)

Italy offers three flat tax regimes for foreigners — €300k for HNWIs, 7% for retirees in Southern Italy, and the Regime Forfettario for freelancers. Complete guide for Americans including US tax implications and the 2026 updates.

Italy has built one of the most aggressive tax incentive stacks in Europe for foreign residents — and Americans who understand the options can dramatically reduce their tax burden on foreign-sourced income.

But the landscape changed significantly in 2024, 2025, and again in April 2026. If you've read an article about "Italy's €100,000 flat tax" that was written before 2025, you're working with outdated numbers.

The Three Flat Tax Regimes: Quick Overview

HNWI Lump Sum (neo-domiciliati)High net worth individuals€300,000 flatUp to 15 years

Regime 1: The HNWI Lump Sum Tax (Neo-Domiciliati)

What It Is

Formally known as the regime forfettario per neo-residenti (Art. 24-bis TUIR), this regime allows high net worth individuals who move their tax residence to Italy to pay a single annual lump-sum tax on all foreign-sourced income.

The Numbers — Critical 2024–2026 Updates

When You Opted InAnnual Flat Tax
Before August 2024 (original regime)€100,000/year
From August 10, 2024 (Law Decree No. 113/2024)€200,000/year
From January 1, 2026 (2026 Budget Law)€300,000/year

Grandfathering: If you opted in under the old rules, you keep your original rate.

Who Qualifies

  • Transfer your tax residence to Italy (spend 183+ days/year in Italy)
  • Have not been an Italian tax resident for at least 9 of the previous 10 tax years

Is It Worth It for Americans?

The US taxes its citizens on worldwide income regardless of where they live. Italy's flat tax regime does not reduce your US tax obligations. You still file a US return every year, still report FBAR and FATCA, still owe the IRS. A cross-border tax advisor is not optional.

Regime 2: The 7% Flat Tax for Foreign Retirees — The Hidden Gem

This is the regime that most Americans don't know about — and it's arguably more valuable for retirees than the €300,000 HNWI regime.

What It Is

Under Article 24-ter of Italy's TUIR, foreign retirees who move to specific small municipalities in Southern Italy can pay a flat 7% tax on all foreign-sourced income for up to 10 years.

7% on your Social Security, pension, IRA withdrawals, dividends, rental income from US properties. Everything foreign-sourced, taxed at a flat 7% instead of Italy's standard progressive rates (23%–43%).

The 2026 Expansion — Major Update

As of April 7, 2026, Law No. 34/2026 (Art. 26) expanded the eligible municipalities by raising the population threshold from 20,000 to 30,000 inhabitants. This unlocked 74 new municipalities across Southern Italy — including mid-sized towns with better infrastructure, hospitals, and international communities.

Newly eligible destinations include:

  • Puglia: Ostuni, Conversano, Manduria, San Giovanni Rotondo
  • Sicily: Noto, Erice, Scicli, Milazzo
  • Campania: Pompei, Gragnano, Vico Equense
  • Sardinia: Selargius, Assemini, Sestu (Cagliari area)

Eligible Regions

The 7% regime applies to municipalities under 30,000 inhabitants in:

  • Abruzzo, Basilicata, Calabria, Campania, Molise, Puglia, Sardinia, Sicily
  • Plus certain Central Italy earthquake zones

Who Qualifies

  • You must be receiving pension income (Social Security, private pension, IRA/401k distributions)
  • You must not have been an Italian tax resident for at least 5 of the previous tax years
  • You must establish residence in an eligible municipality

The Math: Why This Matters

A retiree with $60,000/year in Social Security + pension income pays approximately €4,200/year in Italian income tax under the 7% regime instead of €15,000–25,000+ under standard rates. Over 10 years: potential saving of €100,000–200,000.

The Critical US Tax Layer: What Most Guides Miss

You Still File a US Return Every Year

The US taxes citizens on worldwide income, full stop. Living in Italy on a flat tax regime does not exempt you from filing your annual IRS return.

FBAR and FATCA — Non-Negotiable

  • FBAR (FinCEN Form 114): Report all Italian bank accounts if aggregate foreign account balances exceed $10,000 at any time during the year.
  • FATCA (Form 8938): Report foreign financial assets if they exceed $200,000 for single filers living abroad.
  • Quadro RW: Italy's own foreign asset declaration, required for Italian tax residents with foreign assets.

The Bottom Line for Americans

Before moving:

  1. Hire a US CPA with international/expat specialization
  2. Hire an Italian commercialista familiar with foreign residents and the flat tax regimes
  3. Ideally, have them talk to each other

FAQ

Is Italy's flat tax available to all Americans or only wealthy ones? The €300,000 HNWI regime is designed for ultra-high-net-worth individuals. The 7% retiree regime is accessible to any American retiree receiving a foreign pension, with no minimum income requirement.

Does Italy's flat tax eliminate my US tax obligations? No. The US taxes citizens on worldwide income regardless of where they live. FBAR, FATCA, and your annual IRS return are still required.

Can I use the 7% regime if I live in Tuscany or Piedmont? No. The 7% regime is geographically restricted to eligible municipalities in Southern Italy and certain Central Italy earthquake zones.

Does the 7% flat tax apply to US Social Security income? Yes. US Social Security income qualifies as foreign pension income under Italian rules and is covered by the 7% regime.
