Inheritance and Gift Tax in Italy: A Surprise for Americans

Italy's inheritance tax rates are low — 4–8% with generous exemptions. But there is no US-Italy estate tax treaty, forced heirship rules can override your US will for Italian assets, and cross-border estates need careful coordination. Here is everything Americans need to know.

The US federal estate tax is 40% on taxable estates above the current exemption. Italy's equivalent is 4–8% with generous per-heir exemptions. Americans who know only the US system either assume Italy is equally punishing (wrong) or that Italy has no inheritance tax at all (also wrong).

The true picture is more nuanced — and the absence of a US-Italy estate tax treaty means cross-border estates involving both countries require careful planning that many American expats neglect until it is too late.

The exemptions are per heir — not per estate. A parent leaving €2 million equally to two children means each child receives €1 million — exactly within their individual €1 million exemption. Italian inheritance tax owed: zero. For most ordinary family estates, Italian inheritance tax is minimal or nonexistent.

What Assets Italy Taxes

  • Deceased was an Italian resident: Italy taxes the worldwide estate — all assets regardless of location
  • Deceased was NOT an Italian resident: Italy taxes only Italian-situated assets — Italian property, Italian bank accounts, Italian company shares

This distinction is critical for Americans whose US-resident parents own Italian property.

Americans Inheriting Italian Property

If a US-resident parent owned an Italian vacation home and dies, Italy levies inheritance tax only on the Italian property. The process:

  1. Dichiarazione di successione filed with the Agenzia delle Entrate within 12 months of death
  2. Tax calculated on the cadastral value (typically 50–70% of market value) — a significant advantage
  3. Additional mortgage and registration taxes of ~2–3% for property title transfers
  4. Italian notary required for the real estate transfer portion

Late filing penalty: up to 30% of the tax due plus interest. Do not miss the 12-month deadline.

Italian Residents Inheriting US Assets

This is the scenario with the most double-taxation risk. If you are an Italian resident and inherit from a US parent, Italy potentially taxes you on the global inheritance received, because Italian tax law taxes Italian-resident heirs on worldwide inheritances from worldwide-estate deceased persons.

With no US-Italy estate treaty, the US estate may also pay US estate tax on the same assets. Careful pre-planning of the US estate — beneficiary designations, trust structures, gifting strategies — can reduce Italian exposure, but this requires coordinated advice from both US and Italian professionals.

Gift Tax in Italy

Italy applies the same 4–8% rates and exemptions to lifetime gifts as to inheritances. Gifting Italian property to avoid inheritance tax simply converts the inheritance tax into a gift tax. The same €1 million exemption for gifts to children applies — meaning parents can gift up to €1 million to each child in a lifetime without gift tax.

Italian Forced Heirship (Legittima)

Italy's Civil Code gives certain heirs a legally guaranteed share of the estate regardless of what the will says:

  • One child: entitled to 1/2 of the estate
  • Two or more children: collectively entitled to 2/3
  • Spouse alone: entitled to 1/2

For Americans who own Italian property and want to leave it to a partner, friend, or charity that is not a legal heir under Italian law — these rules can override your US will for the Italian assets. If this applies to your situation, consult an Italian notary about Italian estate planning tools.

The Lack of a US-Italy Estate Tax Treaty

The US has estate tax treaties with Australia, France, Germany, UK, and others — but not Italy. This means no formal credit mechanism for taxes paid to the other country, and no agreed tiebreaker for determining which country has primary jurisdiction over the same assets. For large cross-border estates, this gap requires creative planning through domestic law rather than treaty provisions.

Key Planning Considerations

  • Maintain a valid Italian will if you own Italian property
  • Cadastral value taxation of property is a genuine advantage — the effective rate on property is lower than on financial assets
  • Review US beneficiary designations on retirement accounts — these pass outside Italian succession law but may trigger Italian income tax when an Italian-resident heir withdraws them
  • Consider arrival timing relative to expected large inheritances — becoming an Italian resident before receiving a large inheritance materially changes Italy's claim on it

FAQ

Can I put Italian property in an LLC to avoid Italian inheritance tax?
Potentially, but Italian tax authorities are vigilant about arrangements designed to avoid succession tax. Simply wrapping Italian property in a US LLC and then bequeathing LLC interests may be recharacterized as a transfer of Italian real estate for Italian succession tax purposes. Get specialized advice before implementing this structure.
Does Italy tax life insurance proceeds?
Generally no. Life insurance proceeds paid to named beneficiaries are generally exempt from Italian inheritance tax. This is a meaningful estate planning advantage — life insurance can pass wealth to Italian-resident beneficiaries without succession tax, unlike most other asset types.
How long does Italian inheritance procedure take?
The dichiarazione di successione filing deadline is 12 months from death. The actual property transfer and title update typically takes 3–9 months after filing. If there are multiple heirs, disputes, or non-standard situations, the process can take longer. An Italian notary is essential for real property transfers.
