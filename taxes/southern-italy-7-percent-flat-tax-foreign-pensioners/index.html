Southern Italy's 7% Flat Tax for Foreign Pensioners: The Complete 2025 Guide

Italy offers foreign retirees who move to qualifying southern towns a flat 7% tax rate on all foreign-source income for up to 10 years. For American retirees with Social Security, pensions, or IRA distributions, this is one of Europe's most powerful tax incentives. Updated 2025.

Quick Answer

Italy offers foreign pensioners who move to a qualifying southern Italian municipality a flat 7% tax rate on all foreign-source income for up to 10 years. An American retiree receiving Social Security, pension, IRA distributions, or investment income pays a flat 7% Italian tax — instead of Italy's progressive IRPEF rates of up to 43%. The regime applies to municipalities in 8 eligible southern regions with fewer than 20,000 inhabitants. This is one of the most powerful tax incentives in Europe for American retirees.

What Is the 7% Regime?

Introduced by Legge 145/2018 and codified in Article 24-ter of Italy's TUIR (Testo Unico delle Imposte sui Redditi), the regime per pensionati esteri (foreign pensioner regime) was created specifically to attract retirees to depopulating southern Italian towns.

The core benefit: all your foreign-source income is taxed at a flat 7% for 10 years, regardless of how much you earn. No progressive brackets. No separate taxes on dividends, capital gains, or rental income from abroad.

This is distinct from Italy's other flat tax (the €100,000 lump sum for high-net-worth individuals). The 7% regime is specifically for pensioners and has no minimum wealth requirement.

Who Qualifies?

To benefit from the 7% regime, you must:

  1. Be a foreign pensioner: receive a pension from a foreign public or private entity. US Social Security, federal government pensions, corporate pensions, and equivalent retirement income all qualify. The Agenzia delle Entrate has confirmed that private US pensions qualify.
  2. Not have been an Italian resident for at least 5 years before the year you apply.
  3. Move to a qualifying municipality: a comune in one of the 8 eligible southern regions with a population under 20,000 inhabitants.
  4. Establish Italian tax residency in that qualifying municipality.

What Income Is Covered?

The 7% rate applies to all foreign-source income, including:

  • US Social Security benefits
  • US federal/state/private pension payments
  • IRA and 401(k) distributions
  • Dividends from US stocks and funds
  • Capital gains from US investments
  • Rental income from US properties
  • Interest from US bank accounts

Italian-source income is NOT covered — if you work in Italy, earn Italian rental income, or have Italian investments, those are taxed under normal Italian progressive rates.

The US-Italy Tax Treaty Interaction

The USA taxes its citizens on worldwide income regardless of where they live. Moving to Italy does not eliminate your US tax obligation.

Under the US-Italy tax treaty and the Foreign Tax Credit (FTC) mechanism:

  • You pay 7% to Italy on your foreign income
  • You can generally use the Italian taxes paid as a credit against your US tax liability
  • The net result depends on your specific income sources and amounts

Social Security is a special case: under the US-Italy treaty, US Social Security benefits are taxable only in the US, not in Italy. This means your Social Security may be excluded from the Italian 7% calculation entirely — a significant benefit. Confirm this with a US-Italy dual-qualified tax advisor.

⚠ Important

Before moving, model your specific tax situation with both a US CPA familiar with expat taxation AND an Italian commercialista. The interaction between the two systems requires professional analysis for your specific income mix.

How to Apply for the 7% Regime

Step 1: Move to a Qualifying Municipality

Establish physical residence and register at the local anagrafe. Your registered address must be in a qualifying southern municipality under 20,000 inhabitants.

Step 2: Establish Italian Tax Residency

Italian tax residency is established when you spend more than 183 days per year in Italy OR when you are registered in the Italian anagrafe. Both trigger Italian tax residency.

Step 3: File Your First Italian Tax Return

The 7% regime is elected on your first Italian tax return (Modello Redditi PF) after becoming an Italian resident. The election is made by checking the relevant box (opzione per il regime fiscale di cui all'art. 24-ter TUIR).

Step 4: Pay the 7% Substitutive Tax

The tax is paid as a sostitutiva (substitute tax) — it replaces IRPEF, regional income tax, and municipal income tax on your foreign income. You pay it in a single annual payment using the F24 form (tax code 1836).

How Long Does the Regime Last?

The regime lasts for 10 fiscal years from the first year of application. After 10 years, your income reverts to standard Italian progressive taxation (IRPEF). You lose the regime if you move out of the qualifying municipality to a non-qualifying area of Italy.

Real-World Example

American couple, both retired:

  • Combined income: $85,000/year (Social Security + IRA distributions)
  • Under standard Italian IRPEF: effective rate approximately 30–35% → ~€24,000–€30,000 in Italian taxes
  • Under 7% regime: 7% × $85,000 → approximately €5,950 in Italian taxes
  • Annual saving: approximately €18,000–€24,000
  • Over 10 years: €180,000–€240,000 in tax savings

These are illustrative figures. Your actual situation depends on income mix, treaty provisions, and US tax credits.

FAQ

Can I still receive Medicare if I live in southern Italy?
Medicare (Parts A and B) does not cover healthcare outside the USA with very limited exceptions. As an Italian resident, you will need either private international health insurance or enrollment in Italy's SSN. The 7% tax savings often more than offset private insurance costs.

Do I lose Social Security if I live in Italy?
No. Americans living abroad continue to receive Social Security. There is no residency requirement for US Social Security benefits.

Can I own property anywhere in Italy and still use the 7% regime?
Your registered residence must be in the qualifying southern municipality. You can own property elsewhere, but your tax residency address must be in the qualifying town.

What if the town's population grows above 20,000 during my 10 years?
The regime is generally protected for existing beneficiaries even if the municipality exceeds the threshold after your election year. Confirm with your commercialista.
