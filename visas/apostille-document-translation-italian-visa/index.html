Apostille and Document Translation for Italian Visas: Complete 2025 Guide

An apostille certifies a US public document for use in Italy. Most documents also need a certified Italian translation. Getting the apostille takes 1 day to 8 weeks depending on your state — start early.

Quick Answer

An apostille is a certificate that authenticates a US public document for use in Italy. Italy and the USA are both members of the Hague Apostille Convention, so Italian authorities accept apostilled US documents without further embassy legalization. Most US documents also require a certified Italian translation by a court-appointed translator (traduttore giurato). Getting an apostille takes 1 day to 8 weeks depending on the state; translations take 3–7 days.

What Is an Apostille?

An apostille (from the French word for "annotation") is a standardized authentication certificate established by the Hague Convention of October 5, 1961. It certifies that a public document issued in one member country is genuine and can be used in another member country.

What an apostille is NOT: It is not a translation. It does not certify the content of the document — only that the document is genuine and was issued by the stated authority.

How to Get an Apostille in the USA

The apostille process in the USA is handled at the state level — each state's Secretary of State office issues apostilles for documents originating in that state. The US State Department issues apostilles for federal documents (like FBI background checks).

Step 1: Identify the Issuing Authority

  • State-issued documents (birth, marriage, divorce): contact the Secretary of State of the state where the document was issued
  • Federal documents (FBI background check): contact the US Department of State, Office of Authentications
  • Notarized documents: the notary's state Secretary of State

Step 2: Submit the Document

Each state has its own process: in person at the Secretary of State office, by mail, or online (some states like California, Texas, New York, Florida offer online requests).

⚠ Important

Start the apostille process at least 8–12 weeks before your planned visa appointment. FBI background checks + federal apostille alone can take 6–10 weeks. This is the most common reason Americans miss their Italian visa appointment.

The FBI Background Check: Special Process

The FBI Criminal Background Check requires its own process:

Option A: FBI Direct (Slower): Submit fingerprint card to FBI ($18 fee) → FBI processes in 3–5 business days → send result to US State Department for apostille (6–8 weeks standard). Total time: 8–14 weeks.

Option B: FBI-Approved Channeler (Faster): Channelers (Identogo, Fieldprint, US Channeling) submit electronically and receive results in 2–3 business days. Then send to State Department for apostille. Total time: 4–6 weeks.

💡 Tip

Use an FBI-approved channeler if you need the background check quickly. The additional cost ($20–$50 service fee) is worth the time saved.

Italian Translation Requirements

Italian authorities require that foreign documents be accompanied by an official Italian translation.

For Visa Applications at Italian Consulates

Most Italian consulates accept translations certified by a professional translator registered with an Italian court (traduttore/interprete giurato), a certified translator in the USA (member of ATA) with an affidavit, or some consulates accept translations by the consulate itself. Always check your specific consulate's requirements.

For Use in Italy (Courts, Registry Offices, Notaries)

Documents used officially in Italy require a sworn translation (traduzione giurata) by a translator enrolled in the register of the Italian court in whose jurisdiction the document will be used. The translator appears before a court clerk and swears to the accuracy of the translation.

Finding a Qualified Translator

For US-based translations: American Translators Association (atanet.org) member directory; ProZ.com verified translator directory; many Italian consulates maintain a list of recommended translators.

For Italy-based sworn translations: search for "traduttore giurato inglese italiano" in your Italian city, or ask your local tribunal for a list of registered translators.

Typical costs:

  • Standard certified translation: $0.10–$0.25 per word ($50–$200 for a typical document)
  • Sworn translation in Italy: €50–€200 per document depending on length

Common Documents and Their Full Process

Birth Certificate

  1. Order certified copy from county clerk or vital statistics office (~$15–$30)
  2. Apostille from state Secretary of State (~$10–$20, 1–15 days)
  3. Certified Italian translation ($50–$150)
  4. Total time: 1–3 weeks (if no delays)

FBI Background Check

  1. Get fingerprints taken
  2. Submit to FBI or channeler (~$18 + channeler fee)
  3. Receive report (3–14 days)
  4. Send to US State Department for apostille ($20, 6–8 weeks standard or 1–2 weeks expedited)
  5. Certified Italian translation
  6. Total time: 4–14 weeks

Apostille vs. Legalization

For Italy — a Hague Convention member — you need an apostille, not a full legalization. Full legalization (notarization → state authentication → federal authentication → Italian consulate stamp) was the pre-Hague process and is no longer required for Italy. Do not pay anyone to "legalize" your documents through the Italian consulate if Italy accepts apostilles.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Starting too late: The apostille + translation process easily takes 8–12 weeks.

Getting the wrong type of document: Apostilles must be on certified copies issued by the original authority. Photocopies cannot be apostilled.

Using the wrong state's Secretary of State: A Texas birth certificate must be apostilled by the Texas Secretary of State, not another state.

Expired documents: Some Italian authorities will not accept documents issued more than 6 months ago, even with a valid apostille. Check validity requirements.

Wrong translation format: Visa applications require certified translations; court use requires sworn translations.

Frequently Asked Questions

My Italian ancestry documents are in Italian — do they need translation?
No. Italian documents used in Italy do not need translation from Italian.

Do bank statements need apostilles?
No. Bank statements are private documents, not public documents. They cannot be apostilled. Provide originals with an English cover letter.

How long is an apostille valid?
The apostille itself does not expire. However, the underlying document may have a validity limit imposed by the Italian authority requesting it — some offices require documents issued within 6 months.
