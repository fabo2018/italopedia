Italy Spouse Visa for Americans: How to Move to Italy with Your Italian Partner (2025)

Step-by-step guide for Americans married to Italian citizens — the family reunification visa process, required documents, timeline, and life after arrival in Italy.

By Fabrizio Boggio
Updated May 28, 202614 min VERIFIED
✉𝕏⌘
⚡ Quick answer

The Italian spouse visa (Visto per Ricongiungimento Familiare) is the most accessible Italian long-stay visa for Americans married to Italian citizens. Requirements: the Italian spouse must earn ~€8,500+/year, you need an apostilled marriage certificate with Italian translation, proof of housing in Italy, and an FBI background check. Processing: 30–60 days. After arrival, apply for the Permesso di Soggiorno per Motivi Familiari within 60 days — it grants full work rights. After 3 years of Italian residency, you can apply for Italian citizenship by marriage.

If you're an American married to an Italian citizen — or to an EU citizen living in Italy — moving to Italy is significantly more straightforward than navigating the Elective Residency or Digital Nomad pathways. The family reunification visa (Visto per Ricongiungimento Familiare) is one of the most accessible Italian visas, and for spouses of Italian citizens, the rules are particularly favorable.

This guide covers both scenarios: marrying an Italian citizen (most common) and joining an EU/EEA citizen in Italy.

Two Different Scenarios — Two Different Rules

Scenario A: Your Spouse Is an Italian Citizen

If your husband or wife holds Italian citizenship (and will live in Italy), you apply under Italian immigration law as a family member of an Italian citizen. The legal basis is D.Lgs. 286/1998 (Testo Unico Immigrazione), specifically the provisions on family reunification.

Scenario B: Your Spouse Is an EU/EEA Citizen (Non-Italian) Residing in Italy

If your spouse is, say, French or German and has exercised their right of free movement to live in Italy, you benefit from EU free movement rules — specifically D.Lgs. 30/2007, which implements EU Directive 2004/38/EC. EU free movement rules are generally more permissive and faster.

This guide focuses primarily on Scenario A (Italian citizen spouse), as it's the most common situation for Americans.

Visa Options for Spouses of Italian Citizens

Option 1: Family Reunification Visa (Long-Stay)

The standard path. You apply at the Italian consulate in the U.S. for a Type D (long-stay) visa for family reunification. After arrival, you convert it to a Permesso di Soggiorno per Motivi Familiari.

Best for: Couples planning permanent relocation to Italy.

Option 2: Enter as a Tourist, Marry in Italy

Some couples get married in Italy (Italian civil ceremony or recognized religious ceremony), then apply for a spousal visa afterward. This creates some complexity because you're in Italy without the right visa while paperwork is processed.

Technically legal if you marry during a Schengen stay (90 days) and leave to apply from the U.S., but timing is tricky.

Option 3: Get Married in the U.S., Then Apply

The cleanest path for most Americans: get married in the U.S. with proper documentation, have the marriage registered in Italy (see below), then apply for the family reunification visa from your U.S. consulate.

Income Requirements: Your Italian Spouse Must Qualify

The American applicant doesn't need to show their own income. Instead, the Italian citizen sponsor (your spouse) must demonstrate sufficient income to support the family in Italy.

Income Thresholds (2025)

Based on the annual amount of the Social Integration Allowance (Assegno Sociale), which in 2025 is approximately €6,947/year:

Family Size Minimum Annual Income Required
Sponsor + 1 spouse ~€8,500/year
Sponsor + spouse + 1 child ~€11,400/year
Sponsor + spouse + 2 children ~€13,800/year

These are minimum thresholds. Having income comfortably above the threshold (2x or more) reduces scrutiny.

Accepted income sources for the Italian sponsor: - Employment income (busta paga / payslips) - Self-employment income (Partita IVA / tax returns) - Pension income - Property income - Business income

Official reference: Article 29, D.Lgs. 286/1998; Circolare Ministeriale 15 of August 2009

Required Documents

For the Visa Application (at U.S. Consulate)

From the American applicant: 1. Completed visa application form 2. Valid U.S. passport (6+ months validity) 3. Two passport photos 4. Marriage certificate — apostilled + Italian translation 5. Criminal background check (FBI, apostilled) 6. Health insurance for the visa period

From the Italian citizen sponsor: 1. Italian identity card or passport copy 2. Proof of Italian residency (Certificato di Residenza from Comune) 3. Proof of income — IRPEF tax declaration (Modello 730 or Unico), last 3 pay stubs, or pension letter 4. Proof of adequate housing — property deed (visura catastale) or lease agreement showing enough space per Italian housing standards

Marriage Certificate Requirements

This is where Americans often run into delays. The Italian consulate will not accept a plain photostat of your U.S. marriage certificate. You need:

  1. Certified copy of the marriage certificate from the issuing authority (county clerk)
  2. Apostille from your state's Secretary of State
  3. Certified Italian translation by a qualified translator

If you married outside the U.S., the same apostille + translation process applies for that country's marriage certificate.

Registering Your U.S. Marriage in Italy

Your Italian spouse must register your U.S. marriage in the Italian vital records system. This is done through the Italian consulate (Trascrizione del matrimonio celebrato all'estero). It requires:

  • Apostilled U.S. marriage certificate + Italian translation
  • Both spouses' ID documents
  • The consulate submits the transcription to the Italian municipality (Comune) where your spouse is registered

This process takes 2–6 months. Start it immediately after marriage — don't wait.

Reference: Articles 115–116, Italian Civil Code; DPR 396/2000 (Ordinamento dello Stato Civile)

Step-by-Step Process

Step 1: Get Married + Register Marriage in Italy

File the marriage transcription request at your Italian consulate in the U.S. Allow 2–6 months.

Step 2: Gather All Documents

Budget 6–8 weeks to collect: - Apostilled marriage certificate - FBI background check (use a channeler for speed) - Italian sponsor's income documentation - Housing proof from Italy

Step 3: Book Consulate Appointment

Via prenotami.esteri.it. Wait times: 4–8 weeks at busy consulates.

Step 4: Attend Appointment

The consular officer reviews both the sponsor's qualifications and your documentation. For spousal visas, consulates generally process these with more goodwill than discretionary visa categories — you have a legal right to family unity.

Step 5: Visa Issued

Processing: typically 30–60 days for family reunification cases (faster than work or elective residency visas).

Step 6: Enter Italy

The visa is typically valid for 6–12 months from issue. Enter before it expires.

After Arrival in Italy

Within 8 Days: Dichiarazione di Presenza

Report your presence at the Questura if staying in private accommodation.

Within 60 Days: Permesso di Soggiorno per Motivi Familiari

Apply at the Post Office using the "Kit permesso di soggiorno." Then attend your Questura appointment.

The Permesso Familiare: - Initially valid for the same duration as your spouse's residency status - Allows you to work in Italy (both as an employee and self-employed) - Fully renewable - After 5 years of legal residency, you can apply for permanent residency (soggiornante di lungo periodo) - After 3 years of legal residency as a spouse of an Italian citizen (living in Italy), you can apply for Italian citizenship through marriage

Register at the Anagrafe

Bring your Permesso di Soggiorno receipt + passport + marriage certificate to your Comune's Ufficio Anagrafe. You'll receive official Italian residency (Residenza), a certificate, and access to local services including: - Italian national healthcare (SSN) - Italian ID card (after residency is confirmed by Vigili Urbani visit to your home) - Local municipal services

Can You Work in Italy on a Spousal Permesso?

Yes. The Permesso di Soggiorno per Motivi Familiari explicitly allows employment. You can: - Work as an employee for an Italian company - Work as an employee for a non-Italian company remotely - Start your own business in Italy (with a Partita IVA) - Be self-employed

This is a significant advantage over the Elective Residency Visa. If you're not retired and want to work in Italy (or continue remote work), the spousal route is one of the cleanest paths.

Citizenship by Marriage: The 3-Year Path

One of the major benefits of this visa pathway: Italian citizenship by marriage. Under Law 91/1992, Article 5, a foreign national married to an Italian citizen can apply for Italian citizenship after:

  • 3 years of legal residency in Italy (if living in Italy) — OR
  • 3 years of marriage (if living abroad)

Requirements: - Marriage must be subsisting (not divorced) - No serious criminal convictions - Adequate Italian language proficiency (B1 level — certified by an Italian language school or official exam) - Apply at your Prefettura (provincial government office)

Processing time: 2–4 years currently (the Ministry of Interior has a significant backlog). Apply as soon as you're eligible.

Reference: L. 91/1992, Article 5; D.Lgs. 3/2018

Common Complications

My Italian Spouse Has Never Lived in Italy

If your Italian spouse was born abroad (e.g., Italian-American who never established residency in Italy), they need to establish Italian residency before you can apply for family reunification. This requires registering at a Comune — which requires proof of accommodation and income in Italy. Get this sorted first.

We're Not Married — We're in a Civil Partnership or Common Law

Italy recognizes civil unions (unioni civili) under Law 76/2016, but only for same-sex couples. Opposite-sex couples can register as conviventi di fatto (cohabitants), but this doesn't automatically confer the same visa rights as marriage. Marriage remains the clearest legal pathway for international spousal relocation.

For same-sex couples: civil union partners of Italian citizens follow the same family reunification pathway as spouses under current Italian administrative practice.

Proof of Housing Doesn't Meet Requirements

Italian law sets minimum space requirements for family housing. The sponsor's home must have sufficient square meterage per occupant. If the apartment is too small, the application can be denied. Check with your consulate: generally, a 2-person couple needs approximately 28–45 sqm minimum (this varies by municipality).
