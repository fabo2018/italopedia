FBI Background Check for Italian Visa: How to Get It Fast (2025)

The total process — fingerprints, FBI report, apostille, Italian translation — takes 4–14 weeks. Use a channeler and an expediting service to get it done in ~5 weeks. The apostille is the real bottleneck.

Quick Answer

The FBI Criminal Background Check (FBI Identity History Summary) is required for most Italian long-stay visas — Elective Residence, Digital Nomad, and Investor Visa. The total process takes 4–14 weeks depending on method. Use an FBI-approved channeler to cut this to 4–6 weeks. The apostille from the US State Department is the biggest bottleneck: standard processing takes 6–8 weeks; expedited takes 1–2 weeks.

Why Italy Requires an FBI Background Check

Italian consulates require proof that visa applicants have no serious criminal history. For Americans, this means an official report from the FBI, which maintains the national criminal database (the Interstate Identification Index). The report shows all federal criminal records, state criminal records shared with the FBI, and arrests, convictions, and dispositions.

Italy requires this document with an apostille from the US State Department and a certified Italian translation.

The Two Methods: FBI Direct vs. Channeler

Method 1: FBI Direct Submission

Step 1: Get fingerprints taken. You need a standard FD-258 fingerprint card (ink-rolled). Get this at: local police department, some UPS Store locations, Identogo centers, or private fingerprinting services.

Step 2: Submit to FBI. Mail your fingerprint card with completed request form, payment of $18, and a return envelope to: FBI CJIS Division, 1000 Custer Hollow Road, Clarksburg, WV 26306.

Processing time: 3–5 business days (electronic cards) or up to 3 months (paper cards). Total time for FBI Direct: 2–10 weeks.

Method 2: FBI-Approved Channeler (Recommended)

FBI-approved channelers are private companies authorized to submit fingerprints electronically and receive results within 2–3 business days.

Top channelers:

  • Identogo (identogo.com) — most locations nationally; in-person fingerprinting required
  • Fieldprint (fieldprint.com) — nationwide locations
  • US Channeling (uschanneling.com) — some options without in-person visit

Channeler fees: $20–$75 (in addition to FBI's $18 fee). Total time with channeler: 1–2 weeks for FBI report.

💡 Tip

Identogo has the most locations and is the most widely tested. Budget 1–2 weeks for the FBI report, then additional time for the apostille.

The Apostille: The Real Bottleneck

Once you have your FBI report, it must be apostilled by the US Department of State.

Where to send: US Department of State, Office of Authentications, 600 19th Street NW, Washington, DC 20006

What to send: Original FBI Identity History Summary (do NOT send a copy), cover letter requesting apostille for Italy, payment of $20 (money order or check payable to "US Department of State"), prepaid return envelope.

Processing times:

  • Standard: 6–8 weeks
  • Expedited (in-person or authorized expediting service): 1–2 weeks

Expediting the Apostille

In-person at State Department: Walk-in service available at 600 19th Street NW, Washington DC. Same-day or next-day service.

Authorized expediting companies: CIBT Visas, VisaHQ, RushMyPassport — typically 1–2 week turnaround. Fees: $75–$150 plus the $20 State Department fee.

💡 Tip

If you are not near Washington DC and your timeline is tight, use an expediting company. The $100–$150 cost is worth avoiding a 6–8 week standard wait.

Italian Translation

After apostilling, the FBI report needs an Italian translation by a certified translator. Look for a member of the American Translators Association (ATA) with experience with legal/official documents. Cost: $80–$150. Turnaround: 2–5 business days.

What If You Have a Criminal Record?

Minor offenses (traffic violations, misdemeanors that did not result in imprisonment): generally acceptable. Disclose fully — Italian consulates cross-check and inconsistencies cause problems.

Serious offenses (felony convictions, drug trafficking, violent crime): will likely result in visa denial. Consult an Italian immigration lawyer before applying.

Important: The FBI report will show arrests even if charges were dropped or you were acquitted. Be prepared to provide documentation of case dispositions. An expungement in the US may not be recognized by Italian authorities — discuss with your lawyer.

Keeping Your Documents Valid

Italian consulates typically require that the FBI background check was issued within 6 months of your visa application. Plan your timing accordingly: get your FBI check 3–4 months before your planned consulate appointment. Do not order it 8 months in advance — it may expire before you apply.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use a state background check instead of FBI?
No. Italian consulates require the federal FBI check, not state-only records.

My FBI report shows an old arrest that was dismissed — what do I do?
Bring documentation of the dismissal (court order, case disposition) to your consulate appointment. Be transparent and let the consulate officer review it.

I lived abroad for years — do those records appear on my FBI check?
The FBI only has records from US jurisdictions. However, some Italian consulates may request additional documentation for periods of foreign residence.

Can I use a certified photocopy for the apostille instead of the original?
No. The State Department apostilles original documents only, not photocopies.
