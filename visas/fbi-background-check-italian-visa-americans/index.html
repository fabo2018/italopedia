FBI Background Check for Italian Visa: The Complete American Guide (2025)

The FBI check is your longest-lead document — 6–8 weeks standard, 2–3 weeks expedited. Start here before anything else in your visa application.

The FBI background check is one of the most time-sensitive documents in your Italian visa application. It takes the longest to obtain, requires a federal apostille (different from a state apostille), and must be current — typically issued within the past 6 months for most Italian consulates. Getting this wrong can delay your entire application by weeks or months.

This guide covers everything: how to order it, the fastest methods, the apostille process, and exactly how to present it to your Italian consulate.

Why Italian Consulates Require an FBI Background Check

Italian consulates require criminal background clearance as part of the visa application process for long-stay visas. The FBI background check (Identity History Summary) is the U.S. federal equivalent — it searches the national criminal database (NGI — Next Generation Identification system) for any federal criminal records associated with your fingerprints.

The specific Italian legal basis: Italian authorities assess visa applicants' suitability under D.Lgs. 286/1998 (Testo Unico Immigrazione) and the associated visa regulations, which require evidence of clean criminal record from the applicant's country of origin.

Which visas require it:

  • Elective Residency Visa ✅
  • Digital Nomad Visa ✅
  • Family Reunification Visa ✅ (typically)
  • Student Visa — varies by consulate; confirm yours
  • Work Visa ✅
  • Citizenship applications ✅

Two Ways to Get Your FBI Background Check

Method 1: Direct FBI Submission

Submit directly to the FBI's Criminal Justice Information Services (CJIS) Division.

How it works:

  1. Complete fingerprint card (Form FD-258) — available for download or at the FBI's CJIS website
  2. Get fingerprinted at a local police department, UPS Store fingerprinting service, or other authorized location
  3. Mail fingerprint card + $18 money order/credit card + cover letter to FBI

FBI mailing address: FBI CJIS Division – Summary Request 1000 Custer Hollow Road Clarksburg, WV 26306

Processing time: 6–8 weeks (can be longer during peak periods) Cost: $18 (FBI fee only) Website: fbi.gov/services/cjis/identity-history-summary-checks

When to use direct FBI: If your visa appointment is 3+ months away and you want to minimize costs.

Method 2: FBI-Approved Channeler (Strongly Recommended)

Channelers are private companies authorized by the FBI to receive fingerprints electronically and submit them directly to the FBI for expedited processing.

How it works:

  1. Locate a channeler-affiliated fingerprinting location near you
  2. Get live-scan fingerprinting done at that location
  3. Channeler submits electronically to FBI
  4. Results returned to channeler and forwarded to you

Processing time: 3–10 business days (dramatically faster than direct) Cost: $18 (FBI fee) + $10–30 (channeler fee) = $28–48 total

FBI-approved channelers include:

  • IdentoGO (by IDEMIA) — identogo.com — largest network; available in most U.S. states
  • Accurate Biometrics — accuratebiometrics.com
  • Sterling Infosystems
  • L-1 Identity Solutions

When to use a channeler: Almost always. The 3–6 week time savings is worth $20 extra — especially given the sequential nature of document collection (you need the FBI check before you can get the apostille).

After the FBI Check: Getting the Federal Apostille

The FBI results letter must be apostilled by the U.S. Department of State — not a state Secretary of State. This is because the FBI is a federal agency, and federal documents require federal apostille.

U.S. Department of State Office of Authentications

Mailing address: U.S. Department of State Office of Authentications 44132 Mercure Circle, PO Box 1206 Sterling, VA 20166-1206

Website: travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/records-and-vital-information/authenticate-your-document

Fee: $20 per document Standard processing: 6–8 weeks by mail Expedited processing: Available through registered courier services (FedEx, UPS) for 1–5 business day turnaround

Expedited State Department Apostille

For faster service, registered courier companies offer expedited State Department apostille service:

  • ItsEasy: itseasy.com — in-person at Washington D.C. office or mail service; 1–3 business days for expedited
  • One Source Process: onesourceprocess.com
  • Rush My Apostille: Nationwide expedited service

Cost of expedited service: $75–150 total (State Dept fee + service fee + shipping)

Total timeline FBI + apostille (standard): 8–14 weeks Total timeline FBI + apostille (expedited channeler + courier apostille): 2–3 weeks

What the Apostilled Document Looks Like

The apostille itself is a certificate (typically a separate page) attached to your FBI results letter, bearing:

  • The Hague Convention apostille format
  • U.S. Department of State stamp and signature
  • The name of the official who signed the underlying document
  • A certificate number

The combination (FBI results letter + apostille certificate) is the complete document your Italian consulate requires.

Does the FBI Check Need to Be Translated?

Most U.S. Italian consulates accept the FBI background check in English — it's a U.S. federal document, and the consulate is located in the U.S. and processes English documents routinely.

Exception: If you're applying from Italy (e.g., converting status), some Italian authorities may require a certified Italian translation. Verify with the specific office handling your application.

The apostille itself requires no translation — apostilles are standardized international documents recognized without translation.

Validity Period

Most Italian consulates require the FBI background check to be issued within 6 months of your visa application appointment. Some consulates specify 3 months; a few accept up to 12 months.

Always verify with your specific consulate. The safe approach: order the FBI check 2–3 months before your appointment, ensuring it's fresh but you have time for the apostille process.

If your FBI check expires before your rescheduled appointment (common given long wait times): you'll need to order a new one. Budget for this possibility.

State Criminal Records: Do You Need These Too?

Some Italian consulates request state criminal records in addition to the federal FBI check. This is more common for:

  • Citizenship applications
  • Some consulates with stricter requirements (verify with yours)
  • States where you've lived for significant periods

State criminal records are different from the FBI check:

  • Issued by state police or State Bureau of Investigation
  • Cover state-level crimes (not federal)
  • Apostilled by that state's Secretary of State (not the federal Dept. of State)

Check your consulate's specific requirements. Most only require the FBI check, but don't assume.

Full Timeline: Planning Your Document Collection

Working backward from a consulate appointment:

MilestoneWhen to Start
Consulate appointmentDay 0
Submit complete document package1–2 weeks before
Receive apostilled FBI check3 weeks before (expedited)
Submit FBI check for State Dept apostille5 weeks before
Receive FBI results (channeler)6 weeks before
Start FBI background check process6–8 weeks before appointment

For standard processing (no expediting): Start the FBI check 4–5 months before your appointment.

If You've Lived Outside the U.S.

Some Italian consulates also require criminal record certificates from other countries where you've lived for extended periods (typically 2+ years after age 14). This varies by consulate.

For each foreign country, the process varies:

  • UK: DBS (Disclosure and Barring Service) check
  • Canada: RCMP criminal record check
  • Australia: National Police Check
  • EU countries: Usually issued through their embassy or national police

Each foreign criminal record certificate will need its own apostille (from that country) and Italian translation. This can add significant time and cost for applicants with international residence history.

Presenting the FBI Check at Your Consulate Appointment

Bring to your appointment:

  1. Original apostilled FBI background check letter (original + apostille)
  2. One photocopy of the complete document (front and back if applicable)

Some consulates keep the original; others keep only a copy and return the original. Ask when you book what they require.

If your FBI check shows a record: Don't panic and don't hide it. The consulate evaluates the nature and severity of any record. Minor offenses (old misdemeanors, dismissed charges) may not disqualify your application. Contact an Italian immigration attorney for specific guidance if your check is not completely clean.
