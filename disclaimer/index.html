Editorial Disclaimer

Italopedia is editorial reference information — not professional advice. Here is exactly what that means, and where you need a professional.

Last updated: May 15, 2026

1. Information, not advice

Everything on Italopedia is editorial reference content. It is journalism. It is not — and explicitly is not intended to be — legal, tax, immigration, medical, financial, or any other form of professional advice. You should not act, or refrain from acting, on the basis of any content on this site without consulting an appropriate professional.

2. Where you need an Italian lawyer (avvocato)

  • Filing a 1948-case citizenship petition
  • Property purchase disputes
  • Immigration appeals
  • Inheritance and estate matters
  • Family law (divorce, custody, civil unions)
  • Any litigation in Italian courts

3. Where you need an Italian commercialista

  • Filing your Italian tax return (any form)
  • Electing the flat tax, impatriati, or 7% pension regime
  • Opening or running a partita IVA
  • Cross-border tax planning
  • FBAR/FATCA strategy

4. Where you need a US tax professional (CPA / EA)

  • Filing your US tax return as an expatriate
  • Choosing FEIE vs FTC
  • Reporting foreign financial accounts (FBAR/FATCA)
  • Cross-border retirement-account planning
  • Expatriation (renunciation) tax analysis

5. Where you need a medical professional

Anything health-related. Our healthcare guides describe systems and procedures, not medicine.

6. Our editorial standards

Everything we publish is researched against official Italian sources, verified against lived experience, and reviewed by an Italian professional where the topic demands it. We re-verify articles every 90 days. We correct errors publicly. But we are not, and cannot be, your lawyer or your accountant.

7. Information currency

Italian law changes. Consulate practices change. Income thresholds change in October. We update articles when changes occur, but there may be a lag. The "last verified" date on every article is honest; if the date is older than 90 days, treat the article as orientation rather than legal certainty.

8. Limitation of liability

Italopedia is not liable for any loss — financial, immigration, or otherwise — arising from your use of this site. By reading, you accept this.
